## THE ARCHITECTURE — the fan-out (the deal), and why we got here
**The frame (Jake's, ruled S67): CC shuffles the deck and deals the cards to N blind players. The players don't know what game is being played, so they describe the hand they were dealt — honestly, every card, no folding. CC then reads the actual rules of the game (the question) across all the described hands and finds where the winning cards are — distributed across players who didn't know they held them.**

- **CC = dealer + synthesizer** — the only seat that knows the question. Shuffles the 3,733 nodes, partitions into slices, dispatches blind, then holds the question and cross-reads the hand-reports.
- **Sub-readers = blind players** — each gets ONE slice + the blind prompt, NEVER the question. They describe what each node IS, concretely, regardless of importance. They cannot thesis-match a game they can't see → §3.5.2 forbidden-pattern-3 (thesis-then-match) is made **structurally impossible**, not just instructed-against.
- **The cross-table read = CC, holding the question, reading the blind hand-reports** — this is Perspicillum (assemble) → Pupilla (conclude). The distant-kindred-pair meeting happens HERE, across hands no single player saw together.

**Why this and not a single reader:** This is **§5.3 breadth-before-depth**, the proven Griffin/S54 pattern. The S54 receipt: a single-pass reader passed every criterion while BLIND to the geofence at pool rank 57; only the deep-attention fan-out caught it. **The blindness is split across the wall** — players see the cards but not the rules; CC sees the rules but reads descriptions, not raw nodes. Neither seat can run the gate alone; that split IS the guard. A single reader holding the question AND the whole pool is the exact seat where a thesis quietly forms — which is what we kept circling back to before landing here.

**The road we took to get here (so S68 doesn't relitigate the dead branches):**
1. **Single-shuffle + overlapping chunks** — first cut. Dropped: a single shuffle freezes which pairs can ever co-occur; distant kindred pairs never meet.
2. **Multi-pass re-shuffle ("grandma's redsauce — stir often, new ingredients every time")** — 17 re-shuffled passes, ~175-node chunks, 50% overlap, one Perspicillum reader sweeping all passes with intermediate shape-notes. Dropped: the single reader holding question + whole pool across 17 passes IS the §5.3 failure; the context-wash CC flagged (JC-B) was that failure showing through, and the notes mechanism was a band-aid on a structurally-wrong read.
3. **The fan-out (live)** — blind slice-readers + CC cross-read. The notes problem dissolves (no single reader holds everything); blindness gets stronger (structural, not instructed); it's the proven §5.3 architecture, not a new untested thing.

*Do not re-add multi-pass or the notes mechanism "to not waste the work" — they were the path that revealed the fan-out, not a fallback to return to.*
