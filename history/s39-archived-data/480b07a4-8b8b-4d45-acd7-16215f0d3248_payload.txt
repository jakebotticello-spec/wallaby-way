 This is a common frustration! Unfortunately, Zoom doesn't allow you to change the name "Personal Meeting Room" through any official settings, and there's no hidden configuration file that controls this either.

Here's what's actually going on and your options:

**Why you can't change it:** The "Personal Meeting Room" label is hardcoded by Zoom and tied to your Personal Meeting ID (PMI). It's not stored in local config files—it's set server-side by Zoom.

**Workarounds that actually work:**

1. **Use a scheduled meeting instead** — Create a recurring meeting with no fixed time and give it whatever name you want. You can reuse this link indefinitely just like your PMI, but with your preferred name. This is the most common solution.

2. **Set a meeting topic when starting** — When you start an instant meeting (not using your PMI), you can set a custom topic that participants will see.

3. **Use waiting room with custom branding** (paid plans) — If you have a Pro account or higher, you can customize the waiting room with your own title/logo, which changes what participants see when joining.

4. **Zoom Webinars or Zoom Events** — These allow more customization, but are separate products.

**What won't work:**
- Editing local Zoom config/plist/registry files — the room name isn't stored locally
- Browser extensions or hacks — the name is enforced server-side
- API workarounds — the PMI room name isn't editable via API either

The recurring meeting approach is really your best bet. Would you like help setting that up?
===END===

 Not quite — let me clarify.

When you use your Personal Meeting Room link (your PMI), the meeting will still be called "Personal Meeting Room" regardless of what you put in the calendar invite. The calendar invite is just a delivery mechanism for the link; Zoom doesn't read the calendar event title.

What I'm suggesting is a slightly different workflow:

**Instead of using your PMI link, create a reusable recurring meeting:**

1. Go to Zoom web portal → Meetings → Schedule a Meeting
2. Give it the name you actually want (e.g., "Chat with Sarah" or "Project Sync")
3. Set it as a **Recurring meeting** with **No Fixed Time**
4. Save it

This gives you a permanent meeting link (different from your PMI) that you can reuse forever, and it will display your custom name to participants. You'd then put *this* link in your calendar invites instead of your PMI.

**The trade-off:** You'd need to create one of these for each distinct meeting name you want. Some people create a handful of these ("1:1 Meetings", "Team Syncs", "Client Calls") and rotate through them.

If you really want to keep using your PMI for convenience, then unfortunately "Personal Meeting Room" is what participants will see — there's no way around that, hacky or otherwise.

Does the recurring meeting approach work for your use case, or are you trying to solve for something more specific?

 Sure, here's how to do it:

1. Go to https://zoom.us and sign in
2. Click **Meetings** in the left sidebar
3. Click **Schedule a Meeting**
4. Fill in the fields:
   - **Topic:** Jake Botticello - Lance O's Recruiting Network

   - **Duration:** Whatever, doesn't matter
   - Check the box for **Recurring meeting**

5. Set any other options you want (waiting room, passcode, etc.)
6. Click **Save**

You'll get a meeting link you can use forever with your custom topic. Swap that into your URL redirector and you're set.

 That looks like a Google Calendar event, not the Zoom web portal.

You need to schedule the meeting directly through Zoom:

1. Go to **https://zoom.us** in your browser
2. Sign in to your account
3. Click **Meetings** on the left sidebar
4. Click **Schedule a Meeting** (blue button, usually top right)

That's where you'll find the "Recurring meeting" checkbox and the "No Fixed Time" option. The Zoom scheduler is separate from Google Calendar — Google Calendar doesn't have the "No Fixed Time" recurrence option that Zoom offers.

Once you create the meeting in Zoom, you'll get the link to use in your redirector.
