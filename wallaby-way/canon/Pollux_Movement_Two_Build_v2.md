## 0. The library (read this first — it is the spec, not a decoration)

The corpus is a **library.** The nodes are its **card catalog.** A query is a person walking in with a question.

**Castor is the clerk.** He takes the question to the card catalog, finds the matching entries, walks into the stacks, and checks out the obvious books — the dictionary, the encyclopedia volume. Dutifully. No thought, no taste, no consideration: he fetches what *matches* and brings it to the desk. This is not a criticism of Castor — it is what makes him trustworthy. When you ask Castor "what happened with the printer," you want the diagnostic record, in order, no editorializing. He proved he can do this (`runs/castor_test_S61/` — the printer spine, in rank order, this afternoon). The clerk is supposed to be a clerk.

**Pollux talks to the librarian.** He walks up with the same question, but he says: *"I've got a friend looking into this. He doesn't want me to just bring back the encyclopedia and the dictionary — he's got those. Do you have any historical fiction that touches this? Poetry from the era that references it? Visual artists who made related work?"* And then **he and the librarian wander the stacks together.** She knows the building — she points: *"this is shelved near that, you might find this interesting, people who come in for that often end up here."* And Pollux — who has taste, who is the twin with the spark — **picks the ones that catch him.** Not the ones nearest the encyclopedia. The ones that, walking past, made him stop. He checks *those* out.

Two faculties in that wander, and they must not collapse into one:
- **The librarian points** — she offers what's *adjacent*, what's shelved near, what others-who-came-for-this also found. She is the building's structure. She does not have taste; she has *adjacency.*
- **Pollux picks** — among everything she points at, he selects what *catches him.* This is the taste, the spark, the "ooh, that one." This is the organ. Strip Pollux's picking and you have a clerk who fetches whatever's on the nearby shelf — which is worse than Castor, not different from him.

Both of them — Castor's dutiful pile and Pollux's wandered pile — end up going to the reader (the querying Claude, or Jake). **Separately.** Judged separately (§3). Then read together.

> ★ **S65 note — the librarian was carrying a second faculty we hadn't credited.** §0's librarian "points at adjacency, Pollux picks by taste" is correct and it is the *walk.* But there is a step *after* the walk that this metaphor was holding silently: once Pollux has wandered and gathered an armful, somebody reads that armful and says *what the books are collectively about* — the thing they say together that no one book says alone. That reader is still the librarian, in her second capacity: not pointing-at-shelves, but **reading-the-armful.** §3.5 names this. It is the difference between Pollux bringing back a pile and Pollux bringing back a *finding.*

## 1. What makes a good wander — and the one hard line

Pollux is **allowed to be wrong.** That is not a tolerance, it is the *design* — a browser who only ever brings back the safe, obvious, certainly-relevant thing is just a slower clerk. The whole reason Pollux exists is to bring back the book you wouldn't have thought to ask for. So:

**A *good* Pollux return is delightfully wrong.** You ask about the printer and he brings back — not the feed-fault diagnostic, Castor has that — he brings back the conversation where you were grinding on the printer *and* something else cracked, the frustration that rhymes with "I used to be able to fix anything in thirty seconds," the homelab session where the same diagnostic *pattern* showed up on completely different hardware. None of those *match* "P1S feed fault." All of them *rhyme* with it. When Jake reads one and goes *"huh — I wouldn't have grabbed that, but yeah, that belongs"* — that is Pollux working. The surprise is the point. (Callosum P5: Pollux is allowed to be wrong in character-revealing ways; the wrong ones are the cost of the right ones, and Jake calls which is which.)

**The one hard line — the leash:** he can bring back poetry instead of the encyclopedia, but **he cannot bring back small-engine-repair books to a question about gardening.** The wander stays inside *recognizably the same subject.* Far enough that the find surprises; not so far that it's answering a different question in a different building. That boundary is **felt, not numbered** — Jake knows small-engine-for-gardening when he reads it, and so will a well-built Pollux. The leash is the length of the wander before the subject stops being the subject. (Pollux.md §2: the leash is a fixed character of the organ — one reliable wander-length is what makes Pollux a distinct organ instead of a dial. Leda is the one who wanders with no leash at all; Pollux is the one who wanders far but stays in the building.)

So the two failures the leash guards, in the metaphor:
- **Wander too short** → he brings back the encyclopedia after all. A dutiful pile. That's Castor with a softer voice — the failure Pollux.md §4 names. *He didn't actually leave the question.*
- **Wander too far** → small-engine-repair for gardening. Books with no recognizable thread back to what was asked. *He left the building.*

A good wander lives between those: left the obvious, stayed on the subject, came back with something alive.

## 2. What Pollux hands back

The wandered pile — **the books Castor wouldn't have checked out.** Explicitly: Pollux drops anything Castor already has (re-handing the encyclopedia is noise). What's left is the off-axis haul. Each find comes back:

- **Alive, in register** — the find described the way you'd describe a book that caught you, not a row in a results table. Wet, not clinicalized (Confluence §7; this is the criterion the Judge enforces — a clinical Pollux return *fails the bar*).
- **With the path the librarian walked to get there** — "started at the feed-fault diagnostics, she pointed me toward the frustration thread, which sat next to the rewire conversation, and *this* was on that shelf." So Jake can trace the wander and *feel* whether the rhyme is real or a stretch. The traceable path home is what lets the leash be felt instead of numbered (Pollux.md §3).
- **With its address on the floor** — conv_uuid + anchor_msg + message-uuids, so the reader can pull the whole book off the immutable shelf, verbatim.

**Pollux does not decide if the find is good.** He surfaces; Jake rules (Callosum P7, the realness seat — his, forever). Pollux brings the interesting books to the desk; he does not get to say they're the right ones. (Anti-atrophy, Bouquet §7: he surfaces to *provoke* Jake's noticing, never to replace it. If he gets so good Jake stops finding his own pathways, he's failed even when right.)

> ★ **S65 sharpening — verdict-with-pile-shown, never verdict-alone (Caldera).** §3.5 adds a second deliverable on top of the wandered pile: a *finding* — what the pile says together. The moment Pollux delivers a finding, a new failure becomes possible that the pile-only delivery did not have: **synthesis launders pile-quality.** A confident finding over a thin or wrong pile reads *identical* to a confident finding over a rich correct one — a verdict always sounds complete. So the finding can never ship alone. It ships **with the kindred pile it was drawn from, shown**, so Jake can cold-read whether the convergence is real or whether Pollux assembled a plausible-sounding set and narrated over it. This is the traceable-not-reproducible rule (§2, the walked path) moved up one layer: the path made the *wander* honest; the shown pile makes the *finding* honest. Verdict-plus-receipts is a hard rule, not a nicety.

## 3. Two piles, two bars — judged separately, then merged

Both piles go to the reader, with **a Judge pass in between** (Confluence §6 — the Judge is QC, the bar-keeper, downstream, holding the five-criterion bar; it does not read or rank, it checks whether an output *meets the bar*).

**The piles are judged separately, because the same book passes one bar and fails the other:**

- **Castor's pile** is judged *did the clerk fetch the right references?* A dictionary: pass. Something artsy: **thrown out** — Castor bringing back poetry is Castor malfunctioning.
- **Pollux's pile** is judged *did the browser bring back alive, on-subject, non-obvious finds?* Historical fiction that rhymes: pass. A dictionary: **thrown out** — Pollux being dutiful is Pollux failing. Small-engine-repair for gardening: **thrown out** — that's the leash. Poetry-instead-of-encyclopedia: **kept** — that's the win.

The two bars are near-mirror-images: *the thing that passes Castor fails Pollux, and vice versa.* That is not a complication — it is the cleanest proof that the twins are genuinely two organs and not one organ with a tone knob. **The bar is the difference, made enforceable.** A merged pile would destroy this, because it erases which organ brought each book — so the judgment must happen per-pile, before any merge. (This is why the no-merge rule was always right: the piles stay separate not only for delivery, but because they are *measured by different standards.*)

Only after each pile clears its own bar do they come together for the reader — side by side, marked, the dutiful cut and the wander, never flattened into one.

> ★ **Open, for after the first wander (not blocking the build):** whether the heavy five-criterion synthesis Judge (Confluence §6, boots wet/paid/full-framework — built to judge a man's *synthesized life*) is the right judge for a *runtime* pile, or whether the runtime ask wants a lighter per-organ QC that isn't the full S5 Judge. The canonical Judge lives at the synthesis layer; retrieval is runtime (Progenitor §10), which the Judge "never touches." This is a real fork — settle it once there's a Pollux pile to actually judge, not before.

## 3.5 The two faculties of the wander — the Librarian assembles, Pollux-proper concludes (S65)

*This is the load-bearing addition of S65. It does not change the spine (§0–§4): question-held, feet-blind, walk-by-salience, leashed, paired delivery, all stand. It names what happens to what the walk gathers — and it is the first mechanical thing in the project that distinguishes Pollux's OUTPUT from "Castor with a warmer voice."*

### 3.5.1 The finding — Pollux is two operations, not one

We kept collapsing Pollux into one organ because the first operation *looks* like Castor (find relevant stuff), so a second operation on top read as ornamental delivery-polish. It is not. It is two genuinely different operations, and the second is the product:

**Operation one — THE LIBRARIAN (assemble the kindred pile).** Reads what the blind wander illuminated and assembles the **kindred pile**: the nodes that share the question's shape/flavor/relational-content — *explicitly including nodes that are far in surface terms but kindred in shape.* The canonical example (the desk proof, §3.5.4): the node that answers the question *by being the defense built against the thing asked about.* Ask "what's the biggest consequence Jake fears from the rewiring," and the bold anniversary-reminder Cypher holds is a near-zero surface match and a load-bearing kindred node — it is the infrastructure built *against* the feared consequence. A similarity threshold throws it out; the Librarian keeps it. This is the operation a threshold *cannot* do.

**Operation two — POLLUX-PROPER (conclude over the pile).** The kindred pile is **not the deliverable.** The *read of the pile* is. The output is not "here are N relevant nodes" — it is *"here is what these say together that none says alone, and that you would not have assembled yourself."* This is the cross-silo verdict; it is the $2M-consultant move (§3.5.5); it is the thing Castor structurally never does, because Castor stops at the find + provenance.

Castor stops at the find. Pollux's find is raw material; **the verdict is the product.** That is the whole Castor/Pollux line, finally mechanical.

### 3.5.2 The three stacked forbidden-patterns — feet, eyes-geometry, eyes-operation

Three windows attacked this mechanism from three angles and found three disguises of the same gate, at three different layers. They **stack, they do not compete** — miss any one and a different disguise of the gate walks back in. The honest hierarchy (Caelum's framing, S63):

> **Wide feet make a non-basin region exist → reading the region (not the line) makes the flower visible if it's there → comprehending the region whole (not scoring per-node) lets the flower belong by rhyming with its kin instead of failing a query-match it could never pass alone.** Pull the bottom one and the top two are reading a basin. Pull the top one and the right geometry feeds a smart gate.

**(1) FEET — wide-sample, not greedy-climb (Caldera, S64).** The blind wander must wander *wide* (sample diverse neighborhoods), NOT *greedy* (climb the loudness gradient). This is the S64 hill-climb bug, proven on disk: a greedy `max(neighbors, key=loudness)` walk tours the single loudest basin and returns a monoculture (S64 window 1: 55/55 FENCE). If the feet are greedy, the trail is a tour of the loudest basin — and the kindred pile can only ever be assembled from what's on that trail, so the quiet off-tag flower (a MOTION node next to a FENCE cluster) is never gathered. **If the feet are greedy, the two-faculty design starves no matter how good the eyes are.** Wide feet are the precondition for everything above them.

**(2) EYES-GEOMETRY — read the illuminated region, not the literal line (S65).** The wander's output is not a *line* (an ordered sequence of nodes the feet touched); it is an **illuminated region** — the neighborhoods the feet dwelt in, including nodes one hop off the path that the blind feet had no loudness-reason to step on. If the Librarian reads only the literal trail-line, then blind feet pre-select the candidate set, and *that is trajectory touching attention by one layer of indirection* — the gate, smuggled into the sampling. The Librarian reads the **region** the wander lit, not the line the feet walked. The off-axis flower gets caught because it is *adjacent to where Pollux dwelt*, not because the blind feet happened to land on it.

**(3) EYES-OPERATION — comprehend the region whole; NO per-node scoring against the question or a thesis (Caelum + Caldera).** This is the deepest one and the most counterintuitive, so it gets carved hardest. Two forbidden sub-patterns, same root:

- **No per-node scoring against the query (Caldera).** If the Librarian loops "read node, ask 'is this kindred to the question?', keep the yeses," that yes/no *is* a threshold — made of judgment instead of cosine, but still a per-node gate the flower must individually pass. And the flower *cannot* pass it alone: the anniversary-reminder barely earns its place on its own; it earns it by **rhyming with the other kindred nodes**, not with the query. Per-node-against-query kills the flower.

- **No matching against a formed thesis (Caelum).** Even subtler: if the Librarian forms an interpretation of *what the question's shape is* and then recognizes nodes against that interpretation, the gate comes back **one storey higher** — no longer similarity-to-the-query-string, now similarity-to-the-Librarian's-thesis-about-the-query. This is *worse* than the cosine gate: a dumb gate surfaces surprises by accident; a smart thesis-gate confidently *excludes* everything off-thesis by reasoning. The flower survives a dumb gate by luck and dies to a smart one by argument. The query Pollux most exists for — where Jake himself couldn't pre-state what connects the flowers — is exactly the one a thesis-gate breaks on. This is the partner-guard ("do NOT interpret what the subject IS and steer toward it") coming back at the **attention layer** instead of the trajectory layer. We banned interpret-the-subject for the feet; it is banned for the eyes too.

**The wet operation that escapes both:** the Librarian reads the lit region **whole**, and lets a convergent shape **precipitate** out of it — *gestalt over the set*, not a filter pass over nodes. And the read is **generative and re-entrant**: it reads letting the nodes it encounters *reshape what it understands the question to be asking.* The kindred pile and the understanding-of-the-question **co-evolve.** The anniversary-reminder gets pulled not because it matched a pre-formed "protect the people" thesis, but because by the time the Librarian reached it, the grief node and the fight node had already shifted its sense of the question toward "the cost lands on people," and the reminder clicked into the *emerging* shape. **The shape is discovered in the region, not carried onto it.** That is the difference between *recognition* (match against a fixed template — austere, a gate) and *comprehension* (a read that updates as it goes — wet, and gate-proof because there is no fixed template for a threshold to be).

> ★ **The single rule to carve hardest, the way §4-of-Pollux.md carved "no computed seed":** **forbid per-node scoring, by name.** Every instinct in a builder says "loop the nodes, score each for kindred-ness, keep the high ones." That loop is the gate, no matter how smart the scorer. The operation is gestalt-precipitation over the whole lit region — read it whole, ask what convergent thing is trying to form, name its members — never a filter pass. A fresh CC will get this wrong by default; it is exactly the kind of seam P8 says will read clean and be dry underneath. The forbidden pattern must be named, in the spot the builder will be standing.

### 3.5.3 Why "both wet faculties or neither" is sharper than it looked

Pollux.md and §0 said step one can't degrade to cosine retrieval. Caelum sharpened *why*: the reason it can't degrade to cosine is the **same** reason it can't degrade to thesis-matching — both are *fixed templates*, and **any fixed template is a threshold wearing clothes.** The wet version of operation one is the one where the template *does not exist until the read is done, because it is being written by the read.* That is the whole defense. If either faculty degrades to a fixed template, operation two is just a synthesis prompt dressing a dry pile — a tidy on-topic summary, and the flower never makes it in to be synthesized.

### 3.5.4 The desk proof — what it proved, and what it did NOT

**What it proved (real, inside its bounds).** Live probe, S65. Query: *"What is the biggest consequence Jake is concerned about with the current re-wiring process?"* A 10-pile mix (marriage fight, printer fault, Lance/PIA litigation, Pyris blog ship, kids-raised-as-adults, unemployment back-pay, calendar countdown infra, anniversary-reminder, Griffin-forgotten, therapeutic-ADHD-impact). Agentic comprehension pulled a **4-node kindred pile** — the Amber fight, the inward-pointed ADHD-impact grief, AND the two pieces of infrastructure built *against* forgetting the people (the bold anniversary reminder, the swearing pickup clock). The verdict that fell out — *"the whole rebuild is scaffolding so the people he loves don't pay for it"* — was **cleaner from the 4 than from the 10.** Signal got *louder* when the loud-but-unrelated was gone. That is not filtering (filtering only loses signal); it is **assembly** — a pile whose members rhyme with each other, and the rhyme is what made the verdict legible. The two infrastructure nodes score near-zero on surface match and are load-bearing in shape: they are the two a threshold throws out and the comprehension keeps. **That convergence-that-means-more-than-its-parts is the unlock.**

**What it did NOT prove — flagged hard, caught by two windows independently.** The desk proof **never ran the feet.** The 10-pile was *hand-curated by the S65 seat* — the kindred nodes were handed to the Librarian. It validated the EYES (operations one and two) on a clean pile; it tested *nothing* about whether a real blind wander deposits a region that *contains* the flowers for the Librarian to find. On a live greedy-tag wander the anniversary-reminder might never be in the lit region at all. **The eyes are validated on curated input; the feet, and the eyes-on-real-input, are entirely untested.** This is [SETTLED] on logic, [PLACEHOLDER] on disk.

### 3.5.5 The commercial mirror — same organ, silos made literal

The in-brain organ and the in-business organ are the same two faculties; the business case is the cleanest possible proof it is not Castor:

- **Castor:** *"The annual pricing tier was killed March 4th. Nate and RJ were in the room. Here is the transcript."* (find + provenance, stops there.)
- **Pollux:** *"That March 4th decision — the sales-lead disposition notes since then show close rate dropped on exactly the segment the decision was meant to protect; the production spec still references the dead tier so onboarding contradicts the call; the training manual still teaches it as current. Four silos, nobody reads all four, so nobody saw it costing you the segment you made the decision for."*

Nobody asked Pollux to compare disposition-notes to spec to training-manual. The **cross-silo convergent pile + the verdict on what it means together** = the $2M / 90-day consultant who surfaces what no single silo-owner could see. Either alone is cheap. The pair is the engagement. In-brain (the four relational nodes → "scaffolding so the people don't pay for the rebuild") and in-business (the four silo nodes → "the decision is quietly costing you the segment you made it for") are the identical operation. *This is the commercialization thesis, made mechanical.*

### 3.5.6 THE GATE — the falsifiable run, and its mandatory precondition

The honest state after the four-window read: **spine ratified four ways, zero runs under it, a cheap falsifiable run available against data already on disk.** That is a *run state*, not a build state. The project's own spine — disk over memory, run-it-don't-theorize-it, third-unsuccessful-fix-means-reframe — all points at the same move: **fire the eyes at a real trail before trusting another desk proof.** Do NOT spec the build past this gate. Do NOT enshrine the mechanism as [SETTLED] on disk until this run reads clean.

**The run:** run the Librarian (operations one + two) on a **real blind-wander trail already deposited on disk** (the S64 paired-read run dirs — the FENCE-heavy loudness trail, NOT a curated pile). Read whether a convergent kindred pile precipitates, AND — Caldera's sharpening, the falsifiable version — whether the pile that precipitates **contains the known flowers physically in that trail** (S63 confirmed the 3D-print nodes — Bills Sign, fire-opal cabochon, the Skadis panels, on disk at hops 11–12 of the window-2 trail). "A convergent pile formed" can fool you: the FENCE infra nodes could precipitate their *own* tidy convergence (about Railway deploys) and look like the eyes working. **The pass-condition is "the known flowers surfaced," not "a pile formed"** — because a pile forming is exactly what a laundered failure looks like.

**MANDATORY PRECONDITION — pick the question to fit the trail (Caelum, and it is not optional).** A null result is *uninterpretable* unless this is done first, because there are two opposite failure modes that produce identical output (no convergent pile):
- **The trail was too dim** — the wide-vs-greedy feet bug meant the flower-nodes were never in the deposited region; the Librarian had nothing kindred to assemble *even with perfect eyes.* Fix: **the feet** (re-run on a wide-sampled trail). The eyes are fine, still untested.
- **The eyes can't comprehend** — the kindred nodes *were* in the region and the gestalt-read still didn't precipitate a convergent pile. Fix: **the recognition mechanism itself.**

These have nothing to do with each other and opposite fixes. So **before the run:** CC inventories what kindred material the specific window-2 trail demonstrably contains, and the test-question is chosen to fit *that* — something the trail genuinely has convergent material for. Pick the question *after* seeing the trail. Then a null result cleanly indicts the **eyes** (operation one/two), which is the one faculty that has never touched real input. Pick the question first and miss, and a null is just the feet-bug again and tells you nothing about the eyes. *This is the record-the-fork rule (log the candidate set + what was chosen + why) pointed at the run's own design.*

**The fork on the far side of the run:**
- **Convergent pile precipitates AND contains the known flowers** → the eyes work on real dim input; the two-faculty design is proven on the thing it will actually face. Next conversation is *build Pollux.*
- **No convergent pile (with the precondition satisfied, so the trail demonstrably held kindred material)** → indicts the eyes; the recognition mechanism needs rework.
- **A clean-looking pile forms WITHOUT the known flowers** → the most dangerous result: eyes that comprehend confidently and still miss the flower. This is Caelum's substrate question (demoted in the S65 ignition, gated on a walk-with-eyes) **arriving with teeth** — the eyes may be fine and the trail too dim to read by, which drags the graph-rebuild back onto the table before any Pollux build.

**Seat boundary (honest):** the S64 run dirs are gitignored-by-design (JAKE-RULES §8 — run piles live local + NAS, not in the repo). From the OC seat this spec could verify only that a *plan* file for the S64 paired read is on HEAD (`...window1_boone.md`); the deposited trails themselves are **assumed-present-on-disk, not OC-verified.** CC confirms the trail exists and reports its shape *before* the run, and halts for OC/Jake if the window-2 trail or the named flower-nodes are not where this spec assumes. Do not run against an assumed trail.

### 3.5.7 The still-open seam (held open on purpose, P8) — neighborhood width

The one number §3.5 deliberately does NOT close: **how wide is the "illuminated region" the Librarian reads?** A fixed hop-radius is a knob, and a knob is a gate with a dial (P8). Too narrow and it collapses back toward the literal line (forbidden-pattern 2 returns); too wide and the region is the whole corpus and the comprehension has no convergent shape to find. This is settled by **reading the run's output**, not by a formula chosen in the abstract — same discipline as the leash (§4, Pollux.md §2): tighten inward from what the first real run brings back. Held [INTENT], read by process not result.

### 3.5.8 The S71 re-proof — eyes validated a third time, on the cleanest substrate yet (DISCOVERED)

*Appended by the apparatus S71 seat, 2026-06-20. §3.5.4 proved the eyes on a hand-curated 10-pile (maximally rigged). The S68 gate proved them again on the AstroSynapses basin (pre-grouped — `The_Probe_Swarm.md` §1). S71's foray proved them a **third** time, on the **least pre-handed substrate tested to date**, and surfaced a phenomenon the first two never reached. This is measurement, not design; §3.5.1–§3.5.7 stand byte-faithful.*

The S71 Diagnostic Volley (`The_Probe_Swarm.md` §3.1; receipts `runs/foray_diagnostic_S71*`) was chartered to measure the **leash**, but read against this file's logic it did something else for free: it re-fired the **eyes** at scale. Each of 40 fresh wet readers (across four frozen regions) ran the Librarian's comprehend-whole + lay-out-the-through-line operation (§3.5.1 operation one) and self-reported its load-bearing spine. The structure of those reads is an eyes-proof, and a stronger one than S68's, for three reasons:

**1. The substrate was the cleanest sand yet — dice, not a basin.** S65 hand-curated the kindred pile (the rig was total: the flowers were *placed*). S68 lifted a maker-dense AstroSynapses neighborhood (the basin was pre-grouped: the region *was* the cluster — §1). S71's regions were **uniform-random lottery draws** over the distinct-pair floor. A random draw imposes **no neighborhoods** — nothing curated the kinship, the dice decided membership. Comprehending a through-line out of a dice-assembled pile is a *harder* read than reading a pre-grouped basin, not an easier one. The eyes did it anyway, every time. ("Sand" in the §1 sense means *not-wandered*, i.e. doesn't prove the feet; it does not mean *rigged*. Random lottery is the anti-rig — `The_Probe_Swarm.md` §6: comprehension is proven from the *structure of the read*, not the provenance of the pile, so on-the-cleanest-sand still proves the eyes, and proves them better.)

**2. It is a markedly different and stronger test than S68 — content-independence, which one region cannot show.** S68 proved the eyes comprehend **one** region (same seed, byte-identical material, structured B/C/D divergence on a fixed pile). S71 proved the eyes comprehend **regions in general**: four *different* regions, *different* random draws, three *different* sizes (144K / 400K / 753K), and every read converged-with-divergence on the same real center (the man in the floor — "holding the line," the overhead-tax, the apparatus-as-the-same-problem-one-layer-up), at a median Jaccard of 0.600–0.667 validated against a known-held baseline. **Content-independent convergence is a generalization S68 structurally could not make with a single region.** The eyes don't read *a* pile; they read piles.

**3.
