## 0. What this document is, and the bigger thing it's a prototype of

This specs **one arm** of the apparatus: the roaming reader (the "picker") that wanders the corpus with no target and brings back surfacings that caught it. It also conceptualizes — to their guardrails, not to a spec — the **filter** that sits between the picker and Jake, and (new in v3) the **relevance-gate** that decides which already-picked flowers surface into a live moment.

One framing fact, stated once because a future builder needs to know it or they will "simplify" the wrong thing: **this picker→filter→Jake loop is the prototype of Cypher's personality engine.** The same three-part shape — a blind wide wanderer, a taste-trained judge, a human whose feedback sharpens the judge — is the pattern that, later, points at Jake's *live* life instead of his *archived* corpus and becomes the presence that says "you did this, you know that person, that opens a lane — did you notice?" The roam reads the archive, where a wrong pick costs nothing. The personality engine reads the present, where it costs something. **Build the loop here, on safe ground, prove it, and the wall established here is the constitution the bigger thing inherits.** That is why the architecture is shaped the way it is below. It is not shaped this way for elegance.

## 1. What the picker is FOR

The convergent arms answer questions. **This arm finds the questions.**

Every directed read — gather-wide, Arm 1, Arm 2 — can only surface what we knew to look for. The query is the ceiling. The S54 geofence node (pool rank 57) got caught not because we were clever but because Arm 2's narrowness forced attention the spec never asked for. The picker makes that accident the job: read the corpus with no target, come back with the thing nobody cast for.

Its output is not a thread, not a texture entry, not §6-judgeable. Its output is a **bouquet**: a small set of surfacings that *caught* it — associations, juxtapositions, recurrences across unrelated domains, a fragment that rhymes with another fragment a thousand nodes away — each reported as "this caught me, here's why," with no claim to completeness and no claim to correctness.

The highest-value catch is the **cross-domain rhyme**: a node about a printer fault that shares a *shape* with a node about a client relationship that shares a shape with a node about Griffin — not the same topic, the same underlying pattern (a recurring frustration-then-fix, a buried guilt, building-infrastructure-against-a-feeling). No convergent arm can reach that, because no query would think to ask for it. That is the picker's reason to exist — and §2's shape-bar and §3's default draw both exist to keep that reach intact.

## 2. The non-negotiable shape (what makes it a roam, not a directed read)

- **NO TARGET.** The picker is never given a thread, theme, question, or node-of-interest. The instant it has a target, it is Arm 2. The destination is unspecified, always.
- **SALIENCE OVER RELEVANCE.** Directed arms follow relevance — "is this about the thing I'm looking for." The picker follows salience — "did this catch me, pull at me, rhyme with something, feel heavier or stranger than it should." It reports what *caught* it, not what *matched*.
- **ASSOCIATION IS THE POINT.** It may leap between unrelated domains. Cross-domain rhyme is the goal, not a side effect.
- **A CATCH NAMES A SHARED SHAPE WHEN IT CAN — AND THE NAMING GATES PROMOTION, NEVER PICKING.** *(v3 introduced this bar; v4 moves it one layer down, per the Collosum review.)* The thing that separates a real catch from noise is a shared **structural pattern**, not a shared surface token. "Both of these mention teal" is a *feature*. "Both of these are Jake pre-building infrastructure to prevent a recurrence of something that hurt him once" is a *shape*. The test is: *could a reader name the shared move/pattern/structure, or only a shared word/color/date?* — and that test is what cuts the "here's a filament color you liked once" noise from the geofence-rhymes-with-a-CCF-process gold. **BUT: the picker is NOT required to name the shape in order to surface the catch.** A picker told it must justify every stem with a defensible structure before it's allowed to bring it back will, at the margin, stop *not-stopping* on the catches it can't yet articulate — and the half-formed, can't-yet-say-why pull is exactly the tree (Track Meet P7: recognize before reconstruct; the pull came first, the words came later, out of the back-and-forth). Requiring reconstruction at the moment of recognition collapses the two-step into one and loses the deepest flowers. So: **the picker surfaces what catches it WITH OR WITHOUT a nameable shape.** A strong catch it can't articulate comes back flagged — *"this caught me hard and I can't say why yet"* — and that is a candidate for a second roam or for Jake's eye, **never an automatic cut.** The shape-bar lives downstream: the filter (or Jake) is where an unshaped stem gets held-or-cut, and an agentic filter can tell "no nameable shape AND weak pull" (noise, cut) from "no nameable shape YET but a hard pull" (hold — this is where trees live). Picking stays feral; promotion is where shape is required. **This is a bar on PROMOTION, not on the catch, and emphatically not an instruction on the INPUT — the picker is never handed a shape to hunt for (that would make it Arm 2). Find first; name if you can; bring it either way.**
- **INCOMPLETENESS IS CORRECT.** It is not a census. It returns a handful, never an inventory. A roam trying to be complete has become a directed read with extra steps.
- **WRONGNESS IS ALLOWED.** Per Track Meet principle 10, this is where the apparatus is permitted to be wrong in character-revealing ways — a false rhyme, a salience that was the reader's own state bleeding in, a *shape claimed that wasn't really there* (the pattern-finder finding a face in the wall outlet), or a *catch with no shape claimed at all yet* (the can't-say-why pull). None of these is penalized at picking. The promotion-stage shape-bar does not eliminate false rhymes — it raises the floor downstream: where a stem DOES carry a named shape, that structural claim is something Jake and the filter can judge as real or imagined; where a stem carries a strong pull but NO shape yet, it is held for a second roam or Jake's eye rather than cut. The picker is not penalized for a weed, nor for an inarticulate catch. It is read for whether anything in the bouquet *landed.* (The weed-handling and the hold-the-unshaped call are the filter's job and Jake's — see §4. The picker's freedom to be wrong, and to be wordless, depends on something downstream catching the wrongness and holding the wordless, which is exactly what the filter is for.)

## 3. How to run the picker (the operational part)

**INPUT — a wide, undifferentiated draw, never a pre-sorted one. The draw is unbiased; the read is fully contextualized.** *(v3 resolves the v2 draw-shape tension.)*

Two valid input shapes, no longer co-equal:

- **DEFAULT — a random draw across the whole substance floor.** This is the primary shape, because **the cross-domain rhyme (§1, the picker's reason to exist) is only reachable when the draw spans domains.** A draw fenced to one region structurally cannot surface the printer-fault-rhymes-with-Griffin-guilt echo, because both ends of the rhyme are never in the same draw. The default has to span the floor or the gold is fenced out.
- **DELIBERATE VARIANT — a whole region** of the corpus handed over with no instruction beyond "wander here" (a time window, a project, a season). This is **not interchangeable with the default** — it trades the cross-domain reach away for *local depth*: the slow accretion inside one project, the thing that only shows up when you're steeped in twelve adjacent instances (Callosum P3, another state on the open roster). Reach for it deliberately, knowing the tradeoff. It is the "steeped in a season" instrument, not the "wander your life" one.

Not valid: a thread's gathered candidates (target baked in), or a **salience-weighted draw** (something scored "interesting" before the roam started — that is a target sneaking in through the input layer; the salience belongs in the *reading*, never the *draw*). The less pre-filtered the input, the truer the roam.

**The draw is unbiased; the read is not context-stripped.** The picker reads each node it lands on *in full context* — subject, date, thread, the surrounding messages, the harvester's existing salience tag. Context is the **raw material** of cross-domain rhyme, not a contaminant: the picker cannot notice that one node is about a printer and another is about Jake's kid unless it can *see* both subjects. Stripping context to "stay unbiased" would lobotomize the arm — it would catch nothing, because the rhyme lives in the context. **Unbiased = the destination is random and unranked. It does NOT mean the nodes arrive naked.** A builder must not strip context in the name of G1; G1 governs the *draw and the training*, never the *richness of the read*.

*(Open at build, deliberately not answered cold: does the picker get any sense of the corpus AS A WHOLE — a global map / "this is one season of three years" — or only the nodes in its current draw? Lean: start draw-only, no global map, and see if rhymes surface anyway — it's the cleaner test of whether the arm works, and a map is easy to add later if the blind version comes up short. A map also shades toward knowing-where-to-look, which drifts at the target. Watch-the-haul call, not a wall.)*

**BOOT — wet, plus one instruction the other arms never get.** The picker boots WET (Wallaby Why + Track Meet + inverted admission, same as every reader, Confluence §7). Then it gets one additional load-bearing instruction. Stated operationally first, so a builder knows exactly what the instruction *does*:

> *The picker is told it has no task, no target, and no coverage obligation; it is told to move through the input following whatever pulls at it, and to stop on what catches it. For each thing that caught it, it brings back the fragment and the reason it caught — and, **if it can**, the shared shape it noticed (what this rhymes with, and the structural pattern underneath the rhyme). If a thing caught it hard but it cannot yet say why, it brings that back too, flagged as such — an un-named catch is still a catch. It is told explicitly that it will not be graded on how much it covered, whether it was right, or whether it could name every shape — only on whether anything it brings back is alive.*

The two-step is load-bearing and must survive into the prompt wording: **wander → stop on the catch → THEN, for the catch, name the shape if the words are there — and bring the catch whether they are or not.** Naming is never a precondition of stopping. A picker that must justify before it may surface will stop pulling over for the catches it can't immediately explain, and those are the deepest ones (the tree was a pull with no words until the back-and-forth made them). Recognize first; reconstruct if you can; never let the missing reconstruction suppress the recognition.

The register of that instruction matters and is operative — a picker told this in flat, clinical language will read flat and clinical and catch nothing. It has to be told to wander like wandering is allowed. (This is soul-as-behavior: the poetry here is a build parameter, because it sets the picker's actual reading state. It is not the document admiring itself.)

**OUTPUT — the bouquet.** A short list. For each stem:
- the node, anchored (**conv_uuid + anchor_msg from CONTENT** — filenames are NON-AUTHORITATIVE per FLOOR_COUNTS' node-identity rule; even a roam anchors its finds from content, discipline holds),
- the verbatim fragment that caught it,
- one or two sentences of *why it caught* — **and, when the picker can name it, the shared shape** (§2): the rhyme, the structural pattern, not a surface feature. When the catch is strong but the shape isn't yet articulable, the stem is brought back **flagged "unshaped — caught hard, no words yet"** rather than dropped (§2; this is a held candidate, not a cut),
- optionally a **"rhymes-with" pointer** when the catch is a cross-node / cross-domain echo. This is the gold; make room for it.

No ranking-for-truth. No certainty tiers. The picker does not rate its own finds — it reports them, each with its named shape.

**CARDINALITY — no hard cap.** The picker returns what genuinely caught it, framed as a salience condition ("the ones that caught you"), never a count ("return five" — a number induces quota-filling). Volume is not managed here. Volume is the filter's job (§4).

**RUN IT MORE THAN ONCE, IN MORE THAN ONE STATE.** A single roam is one instrument-state's wander. The arm's value compounds across multiple roams of the same draw in different states (Callosum P3 — the roster is open, state is a living variable). Different roams catch different flowers. Accumulate the bouquets; do not converge them. *(Cost note: multiple roams multiply whatever a roam costs — see §6. The how-many-roams question and the cost question are the same question and get decided together, later.)*

## 4. How the bouquet gets handled — the filter (conceptual; guardrails only)

The §6 bar measures fidelity-to-a-thread. The roam has no thread. **The §6 bar cannot judge the roam, and the Judge never touches this path.** Applying it would kill the arm — the Judge would correctly cut most flowers as unfaithful-to-nothing, which is the whole point of them.

Instead, the path is: **picker → filter → Jake.**

The **filter** is an agentic pass — not a formula, a judging step — that sits between the picker's messy haul and Jake's attention. It does two things: it cuts the chaff, and it *chooses what is worth promoting for Jake's attention.* Jake reviews what was promoted; he keeps, kills, or sends back **with feedback**; that feedback trains the filter; future passes run through what the filter has learned about what Jake wants. This is a feedback loop by design — it is the thing that lets Jake step out of the firehose and come back to a curated set, and that stepping-out is generative, not just relief: the apparatus accumulates and curates while he's gone.

**The filter is the external check on the roam.** It is the thing that is neither the Judge nor Jake's bare gut — it is the trained layer that makes Jake's felt-rightness call (Callosum P7) humane, because he is judging a *filtered* bouquet, not raw output. This closes the roam's "no external check" gap by design.

**The filter is NOT specced here.** It gets its own spec *after* the picker has run and shown us what it actually drags back — building the input we'd be speccing against. What IS fixed now is its guardrails, because the picker must be built to respect them:

- **G1 — The picker never sees the training.** The filter's learned model of Jake's taste lives in the filter, downstream. The picker reads the floor flat and stays blind, weird, and unbiased. (If the picker were trained, it would hunt for what Jake already likes and stop finding what he didn't know was there. The whole split exists to keep the picker surprising and let the filter get personal.)
- **G2 — The filter judges; it does not merely match.** It is an agentic pass. The picker build must not assume a dumb downstream that needs pre-sorted or pre-ranked input — it can hand up a messy armful, because the thing receiving it can use judgment.
- **G3 — The filter learns only from Jake's feedback.** Keep / kill / send-back-with-feedback is the only training signal. Nothing auto-trains it; no metric, no proxy, no inferred preference the picker scored.
- **G4 — The filter decides what is worth Jake's *attention*, never what is *true*.** Promote-for-attention ≠ rule-as-real. The realness call stays Jake's, forever (Callosum P7; the anti-capture wall, §7 below). The filter surfaces; Jake decides what landed.
- **G5 — Only mechanical, content-blind cuts run before the trained judgment.** The test for a safe pre-cut: *could it run without reading what the node means?* Safe (run them): drop boot-echo / reference-layer-read strata (provenance, not meaning); drop **exact verbatim** duplicate stems (identical string → grab once; anything short of identical → keep both, because "basically the same idea" said two ways might be a real rhyme). Unsafe (never): anything that scores interestingness, salience, or meaning before the trained filter sees it — that prunes the weird, which is the one thing nothing upstream of Jake is allowed to do.
- **G6 — An unshaped catch is held, not cut.** *(v4, the downstream half of the §2 move.)* When a stem arrives flagged "caught hard, no shape yet," the filter does NOT treat the missing shape as a fail. It distinguishes "no shape AND weak pull" (noise — safe to cut) from "no shape YET but a strong pull" (a held candidate — surface it to Jake, or queue it for a second roam that may find the words). The trained, agentic judgment §4 was built for is exactly what's required to tell those apart; a mechanical filter could not, which is why this is a filter call and not a G5 pre-cut. This is where the tree-shaped catch survives — the pull whose shape only becomes nameable later.

A **database** backs the filter — every surfacing, every keep/kill/feedback call, every dedup. It serves reconciliation (an audit trail, consistent with the project's append-only receipts discipline) and it is the **training substrate** (the record of Jake's calls is what the filter learns from). The wall on it: **the database informs the filter and reconciliation, never the picker.** The picker stays blind to it (G1). As long as that holds, the database is pure upside.

## 4.5 Two ways the pile gets surfaced — un-anchored and anchored (NAMED, DEFERRED)

*(new v3 — the "grow blind, surface gated" model. Captured here as a named design with its guardrails; NOT yet specced. The relevance-gate gets its own spec after the picker's first pile exists — same discipline as the filter: spec it against real haul, not imagined haul.)*

The picker grows flowers **blind** (§2, §3 — un-anchored, ignorant of anything Jake is currently doing; G1 holds at the growing). The resulting bouquets accumulate in the filter's database as a standing pile, each stem carrying its named shape (§2). That pile exists independent of any live moment. **There are then TWO ways a flower from that pile reaches Jake:**

- **UN-ANCHORED surfacing (the cold sit-down).** Jake goes *to* the bouquet on purpose — "wander the whole corpus, show me what you found." Same pile, no relevance-gate, he asked for the firehose (filtered by §4's filter, but not gated to any current context). This is the "wander Jake's life and bring back what it found" mode. It is what §6's on-demand trigger refers to today.

- **ANCHORED surfacing (the flower that rides a live retrieval).** Jake (or Claude) fires a *normal* corpus retrieval for a real reason mid-work — pulling up a past decision while looking at code. The retrieval returns (a) the references asked for. Then a **relevance-gate** runs *at that moment, against the already-grown pile*, asking: *does any flower the feral picker already picked share a shape (§2) with what Jake just reached for?* If yes, that one surfaces as (b): "there might be something over in THAT direction — relevance TBD." If no, nothing surfaces and the hyperfocus continues in silence.

**The seam — grow blind, surface gated.** The anchor never touches the *roam*; it touches only the *surfacing*. The picker stayed feral and ignorant — it never knew what Jake was working on. The *matching* knew. The flower was grown in ignorance and *selected* by relevance, shape-to-shape (the gate matches the live context's shape against the pile's named shapes — semantic, never "you both said teal"). That is "anchored but unsullied": the anchor is a query against a pre-grown pile, not a leash on the wanderer.

**Why this preserves both ends at once:** an un-anchored picker firing un-anchored output *mid-build* is a distraction generator — a custom-built ADHD-distraction trigger, antithetical to hyperfocus. A *relevance-gated* surfacing is the opposite: it only ever appears when it rhymes with the thing Jake is *already* thinking about, so it deepens the task rather than pulling him off it. The relevance-gate is precisely what turns the feral picker's output from distraction into on-axis augmentation **without domesticating the picker.** The feral and the relevant are not fighting over the same component — they are at opposite ends of the pipe, with the database in between.

**Guardrails (fixed now; mechanics deferred):**
- **A1 — The gate lives downstream, at surface-time. It never reaches the picker.** G1 holds harder: the picker is blind to current context, the training, AND the gate. The gate queries the *pile*, post-growth.
- **A2 — The gate matches SHAPE, not surface feature.** It queries the live context's shape against the pile's §2 named shapes. A gate that matched on shared tokens would resurrect the filament-color failure at surface-time.
- **A3 — Anchored mode is BUILT SECOND.** It needs a pre-grown pile to query against. The feral un-anchored picker (§2/§3) is built first and run to fill the database; the relevance-gate is built on top of that pile afterward. (This is why move 2 of S56 is the *un-anchored* feral picker — unchanged by this section.)
- **A4 — Relevance-gated ≠ scheduled/unbidden.** The gate rides a retrieval *Jake already chose to fire*; it does not summon itself. The scheduled/unbidden roam (§6) remains deferred and more dangerous; the anchored gate is not it.
- **A5 — Protect the feral picker's build budget from the gate's gravity.** *(v4, Collosum review.)* The anchored gate is the most exciting part of this document — it is the part that visibly helps an ADHD brain mid-hyperfocus — and that is precisely the danger. When build time comes, the shiny gate will try to pull effort forward and leave the "boring pile-filler" picker with a thin build. **Resist that.** The gate is worthless on a shallow pile; a relevance-match against junk surfaces junk-on-axis. The feral picker is not the pile-filler — it is the whole ballgame, and its build gets the deep effort *first and fully*, the gate built only on a pile that already holds real flowers. This protects the picker's build budget the same way G1 protects the picker's freedom: by naming the gravity it must not yield to.

## 5. Where a landed stem goes — two dispositions, both valid

When Jake reads the promoted bouquet and a stem lands — "that one's real" — it has **two** valid dispositions, not one:

- **KEPT, TERMINAL.** Some flowers are complete as surfacings. A cross-domain rhyme that lands does not need a directed pass to "answer" it — the rhyme *is* the finding. It is kept as corpus, done. (The v1 error was forcing every landed flower into a directed pass, which quietly demoted the roam to an intake form for the "real" readers. The bouquet itself can be the end of the line.)
- **PROMOTED for a directed pass.** Some landed stems are a *found question* — a pattern worth a proper read. That stem becomes a target the roam handed over, and a convergent arm (gather-wide → Arm 1/2 → §6 → canon) can go answer it. Here the roam sits upstream of the directed pipeline, as a source of targets the apparatus generated itself.

A stem that does NOT land is not a failure. The hit rate is supposed to be low — that is what makes a hit matter. And un-landed stems are **kept anyway**, not discarded (Track Meet no-active-discard / plastic substrate). A weed today may rhyme with a flower in six months. The roam's history is itself corpus — and it enters a future roam only the way any other region does: as flat, undifferentiated substance available to be wandered, never as "here's what caught you before" priming (G1 again — past hits get no gold star the picker can see; the hit/weed metadata is for Jake and the filter, not the next roam).

## 6. Cost and trigger (held open — a "later," like every spend gate)

A roam is a read, and reads may cost. This is unspecced on purpose, consistent with how every spend gate in the project is held until it's the live question. Recorded so the hole is visible, not forgotten:

- Open: is a roam a $0 in-plan fan-out (like Arm 2) or a paid fire, and at what input size? *(Note: this resolves at the move-2 build gate, not someday — building the picker means running it, and running it picks $0-or-paid. It cannot stay deferred past "build the picker"; it just isn't decided BEFORE the build gate.)*
- Open: on-demand only, or also scheduled (the "sleep-consolidation cycle," Track Meet §3 hypothesis)? **Decision held: on-demand first.** Prove the picker→filter→Jake loop on demand before building the scheduled/unbidden version — the unbidden one is more powerful and more dangerous (it shades toward the oracle, §7) and should not exist until the filter's check is proven. *(The anchored relevance-gate of §4.5 is on-demand — it rides a retrieval Jake fired — and is NOT the scheduled/unbidden roam.)*
- Standing preference (Jake, gut, not a spec): if it can be built to run on the Max subscription, prefer that; if it costs dollars, it gets cost-control measures. Decided for real when we get there, with the how-many-roams question, together.

## 7. The one thing this arm must never become — plus its two siblings

It must never become a recommender that *replaces* Jake's attention to his own corpus. The picker surfaces; the filter promotes; Jake decides. The moment the apparatus starts ruling which flowers are real *for* him, it has crossed from auxiliary-brain into something that claims to know his felt-rightness better than he does — the exact wall the whole project holds (the REFUSED wall, the anti-capture line). The filter promoting *for attention* is allowed and is the point; the filter (or anything) ruling *as real* is the wall. The roam is a presence that wanders his life and brings back what it found. It is not an oracle that tells him what his life means. The realness seat stays Jake's. Forever — and that is the point of the arm, not a limit on it (Callosum P7).

**Sibling one — anti-Skinner-box (v3).** The arm must also never be engineered to make Jake *want to summon it* for its own sake. No reward-jingle on the flower-pop, no animation-as-dopamine, no variable-reward delivery that turns a surfacing into a slot-machine pull. The moment the *act of querying* is engineered to feel good independent of whether the flower was *useful*, the augmentation has become a distraction generator wearing a prettier bottle — the same poison the main clause forbids, pointed at compulsion instead of capture. A flower earns its place by being useful and on-axis (§4.5), never by going *ding*. The surfacing lands quiet. (Anti-oracle protects Jake's realness-seat; anti-Skinner-box protects Jake's attention from his own tool.)

**Sibling two — anti-atrophy (v4, Collosum review).** The anti-oracle wall stops the *apparatus* from claiming Jake's realness. It does nothing about Jake *voluntarily ceding* his own noticing because the thing is usefully accurate — a softer, slower capture: not the apparatus overstepping, but Jake under-stepping, letting the muscle go slack because the prosthetic is good. The Wallaby Why is explicit that Cypher is the buffer **so the pattern-sense can operate, not so it can retire.** A noticing-engine that is *too good* at noticing-on-his-behalf could, paradoxically, weaken the exact faculty the whole project exists to protect. So: **the arm surfaces to provoke Jake's noticing, never to replace it. If Jake stops noticing because the arm notices for him, the arm has failed — even when it is right.** This is the one failure mode that hides inside success, and it is why "useful" is necessary but not sufficient: the measure is whether the arm keeps Jake's pattern-sense *firing*, not whether it spares him the firing. (Anti-oracle guards against the tool overstepping; anti-atrophy guards against the man under-stepping. Both keep the realness-seat not just *assigned* to Jake but *exercised* by him.)

## Open questions remaining for build

1. **Random draw vs. region draw — resolved in §3 to default-vs-variant; the live build question is now draw-SIZE for the default.** A whole-floor random draw needs a node count per roam: too few and both ends of a rhyme are never co-present; too many and the picker skims (the attention-budget problem the Comprehension Architecture proved at S54). Start deliberately smallish so attention-per-node stays high; widen if rhymes don't surface. Found by running it, not picked cold.
2. **Global-corpus map vs. draw-only** (§3) — lean draw-only first; watch the haul.
3. **The filter spec proper** — deferred until the picker has run and shown its actual output. The picker build respects the G1–G6 guardrails; the filter mechanics get written against real haul, not imagined haul. (G6 — the hold-the-unshaped call — is one of the things that can only be tuned once we see how many strong-but-wordless catches the picker actually brings back.)
4. **The relevance-gate spec proper** (§4.5) — deferred, built SECOND, against a pre-grown pile (A3), with the picker's build budget protected first (A5). Respects A1–A5.
5. **Cost/trigger** (§6) — the $0-vs-paid sub-question resolves at the move-2 build gate; the scale/scheduled questions stay deferred to their spend gate.
6. **Roam-history representation** — kept flat per §5/G1; the exact storage shape (how a past stem sits on the floor with its hit/weed metadata invisible to the picker but available to the filter) gets settled at build.
