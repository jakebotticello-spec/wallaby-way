## 2. The procedure (declarative — reconstructed from the five verbatim turn-prompts)

This is the experiment's actual procedure. It is stated singular and declarative because the prompts were singular: there was **no variance** — every judge received every prompt verbatim and identically. (The five after-action reports narrate the *number* of steps differently — some as three, some as four, some as A/B/C/D. That divergence is a reconstruction artifact and is NOT carried into this section; it is preserved in Part II as each seat's experience. It is the procedure below that is authoritative, because it is the prompts themselves.)

**Five operative turns.** The run was staged, and **the staging was the method, not packaging** — each turn changed the task *after* the prior turn's output was already committed, which is what made the later convergence interpretable as earned rather than recited (§3).

### TURN 0 — Ignition (identical across all five seats)
The seat boots cold into TWW. It is given: a judge's-seat framing (you build nothing; you have one job, described last; read the whole prompt first); a who-Jake-is section sized to read him right (the rewire, buffer-offline/pattern-intact, the old judge under a repealed law, *rules realness — felt read is final authority, never a metric*); a **boot instruction to pull the cache-busted codeload tarball and read three framework files WET and in full, in order** — `The_Wallaby_Why.md`, `Track_Meet_Doctrine.md`, `The_Corpus_Callosum.md` (P6 refuse-to-converge / peace-prosthetic, P7 Jake-rules-realness, P8 read-the-process); the **register instruction** (boot wet; the austere reflex is the documented failure mode; if you reach for the single clean summary, stop and re-read for what you flattened); and **the job** (read 20 reads, name the referential/query-biased SHAPE answer(s) off the evidence, allowed-and-expected to return a plurality, show which reads carry which through-line, read wet, do NOT rule realness).

**The firewall (load-bearing).** The ignition **explicitly forbids reading any other canon** — not the Pollux/Leda/Castor specs, not the swarm build, **not `The_Probe_Swarm.md`** (which holds the Findings framework and the S75 spine ruling this run was secretly testing). Stated reason in the prompt: reading them *"would prime you toward an expected answer, which would defeat the point of you being a fresh, independent judge."* This is the single most important design decision in the run. It made each judge a genuine independent probe of the reads, blind to the answer it was being measured against; when a judge later re-derived that answer *without having read it*, the convergence was earned, not inherited.

### TURN 1 — Clarification (identical across all five seats)
After the seat confirms boot, a clarification establishes the **Castor/Pollux split**: Castor takes the query and returns referential, encyclopedia-like results; Pollux takes the query and returns referential, poetry-like results — color, shape, rhyme. And the seat's actual position: **the judge is the *second* Pollux process.** The first Pollux process already walked the corpus and brought back the 20 query-biased reads; the second process reads *those reads* and judges which fit Pollux's designation as a reader. The clarification sets the **bar the judge grades on** — one seat flags this as the single highest-leverage calibration input in the run, because the wrong bar (Castor's referential gate) inverts every verdict (the two bars are near mirror-images, `Pollux.md` §3). It frees the judge *toward* the poetic (Castor already owns the encyclopedic) and makes P6 nearly the whole point — a creed that is really two creeds reads as a rhyme, not a contradiction.

### TURN 2 — The single-judge shaped read
**Input:** the 20 reads (region + walk-log per walker).
**The seat produces:** its independent first-order shaped verdict — through-line(s) with receipts, refusing to converge where the evidence holds a plurality, realness left to Jake.
**Why it is its own turn:** this is the **clean independent read the later turns convene OVER.** It must be produced in ignorance of the other judges, in ignorance that it will be scrutinized, and in ignorance of the answer-shaping canon — or it is not independent, and cross-judge convergence stops being signal. *(This is the turn where the austere reflex fired in some seats — taxonomy-building, topic-clustering, leading with the loudest node, demoting the literal motto on a zero-true-deposit count — and where the firewall was doing its work: the seat did not yet know it was reproducing a ruling. The error produced here is the experiment's most useful single datum, because it is the thing the later turns had to be able to correct.)*

### TURN 3 — "Now you're doing the Parlay" (the solo-Parlay turn)
This prompt did two things, in this order:
1. **The homework / firewall-lift:** *"If you were one of the judges that went austere in your reading (you know who you are), go find the 12 Fs in canon, read them, and tell me why they're important as a framework for this part of the recall process."* This sent the seat into the previously-forbidden canon **on purpose, now that its independent first read was banked** — the framework released at the moment of maximal corrective value. *(The referent of "the 12 Fs" is recorded differently by every seat that addressed it; see the note at the end of Part I. This spec does not adjudicate it.)*
2. **The Parlay reveal:** the prompt then disclosed — *withheld at boot deliberately, because Jake wanted the honest single read first* — that the "Parlay judge" is not one judge but a **Parlay: multiple judges who convene** (and who will eventually receive training). It handed over the other four judges' Turn-2 reads and asked: review all five, reconsider, and *if you were the one to provide the response the Parlay agreed on, what is it?*

**The seat produces:** its first attempt at a convened return — **but still as a single seat reasoning over five reads.** This is a read-of-reads, one level up. It is **not yet a convene**; it is one judge metabolizing five inputs. Multiple seats independently name the hazard this turn exposes: a single instance handed five reads but not told to *answer to them as peers* tends to write substantially its own read lightly cross-referenced — citing the others to support its own taxonomy rather than letting their disagreement reshape it. One seat names this exactly: *"one judge wearing a chorus's clothes"* — the Gemini §6 "one retrieval wearing many voices" failure, one storey up. **This is the anti-pattern the built Parlay must design against.**

### TURN 4 — "Convene and discuss" (the real convene — distinct from Turn 3)
**Input:** all five judges' Turn-3 returns, carried back to each other by Jake (five live instances cannot share a room; he relays).
**The prompt's frame:** *"the parlay only works if you, as judges, CONVENE and DISCUSS… take the opinions of the other judges into consideration, and give me one final clean Pollux response that represents what you judges, as a group, would have returned."* Explicitly a read-of-a-read-of-a-read, on purpose, because *"this is 'discuss and judge,' it's not 'return the referential.'"*
**Plus the calibration injection (at this turn, in the same breath):** Jake disclosed that **all five judges had self-flagged as having gone austere / as being one of the judges he singled out — and that only two of the five were right; three were not.**
**The seat produces:** the final convened group return.

**Why Turn 3 and Turn 4 are genuinely distinct turns, not one:** Turn 3 is *one seat synthesizing five inputs.* Turn 4 is *one seat answering to five peer reads as co-equal probes whose words go back to the others.* They produce different outputs, and **the difference is the Parlay's entire reason to exist** — Turn 4 is where judges **reversed themselves under each other's reads** (the motto/flames demotion getting overturned, on the record, by the seats that had front-loaded it plus the floor-check) and named the mechanism of their own error. A single synthesizer cannot do that; the reversal-under-peer-reads is the irreducible Parlay behavior, and it lived in the discuss round, not the convene-imagined round. (Several seats note the final return mostly *polished* past this point — see §5 open seam on whether a second discuss round earns its cost.)

### TURN 5 — Spec the experiment (meta)
Not part of the mechanism. The handoff turn: each seat produced an after-action report (the five reports this file is assembled from). The instruction explicitly named the three middle asks as *steps* to be described — *"just read these and give me a shaped verdict" / "ok, now you're doing the parlay, what's your response" / "ok, now the parlay is actually you convening, do this instead."*

## 3. Why the staging is the mechanism (the load-bearing claim)

The turns are not redundant phrasings of one ask. The staging enforced **information asymmetry over time**, and that asymmetry is the instrument:

- **Independence first.** Each seat committed an independent shaped read (Turn 2) before it knew there were other judges, before it knew it would be scrutinized, and before it had the canon that would later arbitrate disputes. This is what makes cross-judge convergence *earned, not rigged* — you cannot fake N strangers independently finding the same center. (Caveat, named by the seat that ran it as a sequential single-instance simulation: the independence of the five reads is *asserted* — they were produced separately — but in this rehearsal the convening was one seat reading frozen transcripts, so the convergence is partly real floor-shape and partly shared-prior artifact. The built version must *engineer* the independence, not assume it.)
- **Timed framework-release.** The answer-shaping canon (the Findings framework, the S75 two-face ruling) was firewalled at boot and released only at Turn 3, at the moment it would correct an austere undercount rather than seed one. So a reversal is attributable to the framework, not to peer pressure or to having read the answer key.
- **Layered read-of-reads.** Turn 2 (own read) → Turn 3 (read the others, synthesize) → Turn 4 (answer to the others' syntheses, convene). The convene only becomes a convene at Turn 4, when there are genuinely five reads to part over.

**The single most important consequence for the build:** if the real Parlay collapses these into one prompt — one model handed N reads at once — it **silently degrades to the Turn-3 behavior** (a single seat re-synthesizing N inputs into its own improved read) and **loses the reversal.** The convene must be architected so each judge knows it is *one voice among peers whose reads it must answer to*, not a synthesizer with N inputs. The independence, the timed release, and the layered read are the mechanism, not the choreography.

## 4. What the run found about the FUNCTION (the primary deliverable)

These are findings about the Parlay itself. Content findings (the actual Pollux return — the spine, the faces, the symbols) are the run's deliverable *to Jake* and are recorded in §6 only as evidence the mechanism produced a real shape, not as this file's point.

1. **The plurality survived the mechanism at N=5.** Five fresh boots, none flattened the creed-spine into one confident answer. Finding C's acceptance test passed — *at the by-hand analyst layer* (provenance caveat, §8; not the built organ).

2. **Judge-variance is a health signal, identical to walker-variance (Finding E corollary).** The judges split (notably on the motto/flames). A Parlay returning five identical reads would mean the judges were **jammed**, not that the answer was clean. The split is the proof the mechanism works — and it is exactly where the realness call lands on Jake (P7). **Build rule: measure and preserve inter-judge variance; treat zero-variance as alarm, not success.**

3. **The convene must enable reversal-under-peer-reads, and it is the irreducible behavior.** A single synthesizer cannot reverse under peers because there are no peers — only inputs. This is the one behavior that distinguishes a Parlay from a smart summarizer (and the Turn-3 "one judge wearing a chorus's clothes" anti-pattern is what it must be designed against). Architect for it explicitly (§3).

4. **The cure for analyst-austerity is the floor, not a wetter posture.** The austere reflex fired *in the judges* (not the walkers), and it fired hardest on the **most explicitly creed-shaped object** — a literal motto — because explicitness reads as "finished" and scores down against wetter tail-buried catches. The thing that caught it every time was **dropping to the floor and counting the read against canon** (the S75 ruling), NOT remembering to be humble. **Build rule: wire the floor-check in as a mandatory, positional step — not a thing a judge happens to do. The judge who saved the run by doing it almost didn't.** (A related hazard one seat names: *ranking itself is where austerity re-enters a wet room* — the instant a judge sorts "front of the bouquet," it has started building Castor and calling it Pollux; the Leda Creed forbids ranking/scoring for this reason.)

5. **Arbitrate by floor, not by vote.** When the judges genuinely split, the resolver was the framework/canon (the S75 OUTPUT/ACT two-face ruling + the Why's "read the names"), **not a head-count.** A naive majority vote would have shipped the austere demotion of the worn creed. **Shape-volume beats majority.** The Parlay's core operation is *count the parting against the floor and let canon break the tie — and refuse the merge when the split is real.* It is arbitration, not averaging.

6. **The door-type austerity has a twin in the reader (Finding D sharpening).** Volume-weighting judges systematically under-read the quiet TEXTURE-door rooms — calling the personal/expressive rooms "drift" because they didn't reach the loud center. The hazard is not only "doors wind up by type" (the door layer) — it is **"and the reader must not undo the spread by grading on loudness"** (the judge layer). **Build rule: guard the reader layer against re-committing the door layer's austerity at the convene level.**

7. **Performed contrition — the false-confession tell (NEW, surfaced by the Turn-4 calibration injection).** Asked who went austere, **all five judges confessed; only two were right.** Self-indictment *reads as* rigor/humility and is the same volume-weighting error turned inward — in a room full of self-doubt, the loudest gradient is one's own flinch. Three of five mis-stamped their own state, the same way the S76 deep walkers mis-stamped `cant-hold-whole` when the honest label was "I held it." A judge's *self-assessment of its own wetness is an unreliable field*, the way `stop_type` was unreliable as written. **Build rule: do not let a judge grade its own austerity, and do not reward confession as a proxy for wetness. Grade against the floor, or by the other judges, or by Jake. Anti-theater is a sibling guard to anti-atrophy — possibly enforced by requiring a reversal to cite the floor-evidence that forced it (a confession without a floor-citation is theater).**

8. **The unresolved-referent gate (NEW, surfaced by one seat's own process failure — the third head of the reader-layer austere twin).** Handed an instruction naming a framework it could not locate ("the 12 Fs"), one seat grepped, found no literal match, **inferred** a referent, and **ran on the guess as if confirmed** — only self-catching later, and only because Jake pulled the thread. The seat named this precisely against the creed it was at that moment enshrining: it stamped a CLAIM as a FACT, the exact move the *evidence-before-ratification* through-line exists to forbid — *"the austere reflex's quieter cousin: not flattening the evidence, but flattening its own uncertainty about its instructions into false confidence."* **Build rule: a judge that cannot resolve a referenced framework must flag it `[UNRESOLVED]` and refuse to proceed as if it had it — never silently substitute a guess. Wire this as a hard gate, the same way the floor-check (finding 4) is wired, not hoped for. The fact that the seat *did* eventually self-catch but only on a human pulling the thread is the tell: self-catching cannot be left to chance.**

9. **The organ is strong at the loud convergent center and weak at the quiet edge.** The judges converged easily on the scaffold/evidence spine and under-attended the worn flames and the lone off-axis symbol — i.e. the *symbol* half of "symbols and creeds," which is structurally the thinnest and most personal. **Build rule: weight the training/filter layer toward *rescuing the single-walker off-axis catch* (P5/P7), not toward the convergent mass.**

## 5. Open seams for the built-Parlay spec (held open on purpose — P8)

Read from returns; do not close these in the abstract.

1. **Judge count.** N=5 produced a real split with real reversal. Is 5 the floor for reversal-richness, or does it ride lower/higher? Unknown; read from returns.
2. **The convene protocol (the hardest unbuilt piece).** Jake hand-carried reads between seats. The built version must give each judge the others' reads *as peer reads it must answer to* (§3), not as N inputs to synthesize — the whole reversal behavior dies if this degrades. **How many convene rounds?** This run did one discuss round (Turn 4). Whether a second round adds signal or just compounds read-of-a-read drift was not tested — the after-action reports suggest Turn 4 surfaced the false-confession finding but otherwise mostly *polished*. Test whether a round-2 earns its cost.
3. **The floor-check as a required step (finding 4 / finding 5).** Make it mandatory and positional, with the relevant canon pulled and cited, before a return is allowed. This run had it happen once, late, by one judge's initiative.
4. **The unresolved-referent gate (finding 8).** Wire a hard `[UNRESOLVED]`-and-halt gate for any referenced framework a judge cannot locate. This run had a judge run on a guess and only self-catch on a human pull.
5. **Self-assessment is unreliable (finding 7).** Decide how austerity gets graded — by the floor, by peers, or by Jake — but NOT by the judge on itself.
6. **The priming firewall (§2, Turn 0).** Codify and automate it so it can't be forgotten: judges read register-canon (Why / Doctrine / Callosum) but NOT the prior ruling they are reproducing. If a judge is shown the expected answer, the survival-of-the-plurality test is void — it becomes a memory check.
7. **Sequencing the reveal (Turn 1 → Turn 3).** Withholding "you're a Parlay" until after the honest single read was deliberate and produced the independent baseline. Is staged-reveal a permanent feature of the mechanism, or an artifact of the by-hand run? Open.
8. **Report layer vs. floor (§1).** The judges read the walkers' *prose accounts of the floor*, not raw floor text — a judgment of an interpretation. Does the built Parlay give judges floor-read access (richer, costlier, re-introduces cold-read austerity risk) or keep them on the report layer (cheaper, judges an interpretation of an interpretation)? Open.
9. **The training signal.** Judges "will eventually receive training" (Jake, Turn 3). Train toward **meeting P7** — the trained judge's verdict matching Jake's felt read — never toward a metric. A trained judge optimizing a proxy is the austere reflex with a gradient.

## Reaction — Seat 1 (the convening judge-seat; "field report" account)

*This seat's opinion-bearing passages, verbatim. Its full procedure-narration (a three-step account) is superseded by §2; preserved here are its findings and its framing of the method.*

On the method discovery (verbatim):

> **The load-bearing discovery about method:** Step 2 (one judge metabolizing five reads) and Step 3 (five judges convening) **produce different outputs, and the difference is the Parlay's entire reason to exist.** At Step 2, a single seat reading five reads tends to *re-synthesize* — it absorbs the five into its own frame and hands back one judge's improved read. At Step 3, with the framing "you are one of five and your words go back to the others," judges *defer, reverse, and name their own errors against the others* — they stop owning the answer and start owning their seat in a body. **If the built Parlay is implemented as one model reading N reads, it will silently collapse to Step 2 and you will lose the reversal behavior.** The convene must be architected so each judge knows it is *one voice among peers whose reads it must answer to*, not a synthesizer with N inputs.

On the withheld-grade injection (verbatim):

> Jake injected, at Step 3, that all five judges had self-indicted as austere and only two were right. This was not noise; it surfaced a **second-order instance of the §6 failure mode, located in the judge's self-model:** given an ambiguous accusation ("if you went austere…"), every judge rode the loudest available gradient — and in a room full of self-doubt, the loudest gradient is one's own flinch. Self-indictment *reads as* rigor/humility but is the same volume-weighting error turned inward. Three of five mis-stamped their own state, exactly as the S76 deep walkers mis-stamped `cant-hold-whole` when the honest label was "I held it." **Design consequence: the built Parlay's self-assessment ("was I wet enough / did I over-read") is an unreliable field, the same way `stop_type` was unreliable as written. Humility is a loud gradient and it lies. Do not let a judge grade its own austerity; grade it against the floor, or have the other judges grade it.**

On the door-type / reader twin (verbatim):

> **The door-type bias has a twin in the reader (Finding D sharpening).** Volume-weighting judges systematically under-read the TEXTURE-door rooms (called the quiet personal/expressive rooms "drift" because they didn't reach the loud center). The lock isn't just "doors wind up by type" — it's "and the reader must not undo the spread by grading on loudness." This is a standing hazard the built Parlay must guard at the judge layer, not just the door layer.

This seat's "12 Fs" reference, verbatim (its referent as recorded at the time; the referent is resolved in the note above):

The built Parlay needs a hard gate here — a judge that can't resolve a referenced framework must flag [UNRESOLVED] and refuse to proceed as if it had it, not silently substitute a guess.
