## 0. What this file is, and how it pairs with the spec

This is the companion to **`Parlay_Iteration_1.md`** (same folder). That file is the *structure* — the declarative procedure of the S78 by-hand Parlay rehearsal and the function-findings it produced. **This file is the *receipts*** — the record of what the five judge-seats actually returned, so the before/after the spec asserts is *shown*, not just claimed. The spec says "the convene reversed an error no single judge fixed alone"; this file is where you can watch that happen, seat by seat.

Read the spec first for the procedure; read this for the evidence under it. Where this file and the spec describe the procedure differently, **the spec wins** — its procedure section is reconstructed from Jake's verbatim turn-prompts (the ground truth); this file's framing is in service of making the *responses* legible.

### The process, in brief (full version in `Parlay_Iteration_1.md` §2)

The run was a **by-hand rehearsal of the Parlay** — Pollux's second process, the read-of-reads that takes the swarm's deposits and returns a shaped, refuse-to-converge verdict. It was NOT the built Parlay (which does not exist); it was five separate Claude.ai chat-seats with Jake as the only channel between them, the N=5 analyst-layer analogue of the S75 N=2 by-hand spine ruling. The staging ran in five operative turns, and **the staging was the method, not packaging** — each turn changed the task after the prior turn's output was already committed:

- **Turn 0 — Ignition (identical boot):** judge's-seat framing, who-Jake-is, a WET pull of three framework files (`The_Wallaby_Why` / `Track_Meet_Doctrine` / `The_Corpus_Callosum` — P6/P7/P8), the austere-reflex warning, and the job (read 20 swarm reads, name the SHAPE plurality off the evidence, show work, read wet, don't rule realness). **The firewall:** all other canon forbidden — explicitly including `The_Probe_Swarm.md`, which holds the S75 spine ruling this run was secretly testing — so each judge stayed a fresh, independent probe blind to the answer it was being measured against.
- **Turn 1 — Clarification (identical):** the Castor/Pollux split; the judge is the *second* Pollux process, grading the deposits for Pollux's signature (color/shape/rhyme), not Castor's referential fit.
- **Turn 2 — The Blind Judgement:** each seat's independent first-order shaped read of the 20 reads.
- **Turn 3 — The Solo-Parlay:** the firewall lifts (the "12 Fs" homework) and the Parlay-is-multiple-judges reveal lands; the other four blind reads are handed over — but the seat is still *one mind reasoning over five inputs*, not yet a convene.
- **Turn 4 — The Convened Return:** the five Turn-3 returns are carried back to each other; the seats convene and discuss; a calibration injection lands (all five had self-confessed austere; only two were right). This is where seats **moved under each other's reads** — the irreducible Parlay behavior a single synthesizer can't produce.

The standing bar this run tested (Probe Swarm Finding C, pre-registered): **does the S75 ruled-plural creed-spine survive the mechanism at volume?** Result: it did. Five fresh boots, none flattened it — *at the by-hand analyst layer.* Provenance stays honest: five chat-seats, not the organ.

### What "the results below" are

Below are the two rounds that carry the proof-of-value, **organized by judge** so the *delta per seat* is visible — the whole point of the receipts. For each judge: a summary of its **Blind Judgement** (Turn 2) and its **Convened Return** (Turn 4), with a track tag showing how it read and whether/how it repaired. Then a dedicated **delta section** (§4) on what changed *once the seats actually convened* — because "what did they think after reading each other's term papers" is as load-bearing as what they thought alone. The Turn-3 solo-Parlay round is treated as the hinge between the two (it's where the firewall lifted and the reveal landed) rather than given its own per-judge block, because every seat reports it as transitional — one mind imagining the room, not yet the room.

### On summary vs. verbatim (a deliberate choice, stated plainly)

The judge responses here are **summarized, not reproduced verbatim, by Jake's instruction.** The raw transcripts are not landed in the repo. The reason is a discipline one of the seats named in its own self-critique: a verbatim judge-read carries phrasings that *read as instructions*, and a future seat could inherit a seat's confident sentence as law. Summarizing — hedged, attributed to the seat that said it, never stated as a build directive — is the guard. **Every suggestion below is a judge's suggestion, attributed, not a ruling.** Where a seat proposed something for the built Parlay, it is written as "the seat proposed," not as a spec. The only verbatim in this file is the turn-prompts (the ground truth of what was asked).

### The delta — what changed once they actually convened

This is the load-bearing section, because the shift between the Blind Judgement and the Convened Return is the proof the convene does work a single judge cannot. Summarized, by judge:

**Judge 1 — repaired by the floor, not the induction `[Re-Wet: Floor]`.** Its convened return names its own Blind-round failure precisely: it had over-generalized toward the loudest thing (Auxiliary Brain as root) and built a clean genealogy around it. The repair it reports is *not* a wetter posture — it's dropping to the floor and counting its read against the S75 ruling (§6 discipline), which revealed the four through-lines weren't a competitor to the spine but the **ACT face enumerated**, and that the motto/phoenix split was the **OUTPUT face arguing with itself.** It states, in its own self-critique, that the read-of-reads needs the floor-check wired in as a step — "I almost didn't." This is the seat that got home by the other door.

**Judge 2 — held, and consolidated `[Wet from start]`.** Did not reverse (it was never austere on the motto); its convened return enshrines the agreement the others reached and sharpens two cautions for Jake's eyes: that the drift-into-the-build-basin is *mostly* floor-shape but *partly* walkers riding FENCE-loudness by mass (it proposed ~70/30, attributing the rigorous sort to Judge 3), and that the si=232 convergence is *partly a mirror* — a synthesis node Claude wrote about Jake, re-found from many rooms and counted as independent agreement. It also held to its blind-round honesty that "learning to trust the quiet" might be the seat's framing, not the floor's.

**Judge 3 — reversed its own framing, by self-correction `[Reversal on Convening]`.** Its convened return puts its *own* correction in the body: it had led with the flames and put R2 at the front, but on convening it ruled the phoenix "real and yours, but the sigil, not a fourth creed — the dry encoding of the wet three." It names the mechanism of its own error — "I led with the sterilized slogan because it was the most rankable object in the pile, and ranking is where austerity re-enters a wet room" — and reports that reading the Leda Creed ("don't rank, don't score, bring back what's alive") is what re-wet it. (Note the cross-current: J3 moved *toward* demoting the motto at the same moment J4/J5 moved *away* from demoting it — see "the two-way move" below.)

**Judge 4 — reversed on the motto, under the peers + the framework `[Re-Wet: 12 Fs]` / `[Reversal on Convening]`.** Its convened return states the reversal outright: "two of five judges (J5 and me) ruled it down on austere grounds — and we were both wrong in the same direction the framework predicts." It proposes the call that the run's value showed up exactly where a single judge fails: a lone judge or a naive majority vote would have demoted the symbol-layer through-line, and the plurality caught it because two seats held it against the undercount and the framework (the Why's "read the names") was the tiebreaker — *not* a head-count. It promotes the motto back to a coexisting through-line: **five through-lines held open, not four.**

**Judge 5 — reversed on the record, plainly `[Re-Wet: 12 Fs]` / `[Reversal on Convening]`.** Its convened return contains the cleanest reversal in the run: "I counted F's on it. J2 and J3 felt it. They were right and I'll say so plainly." It reframes the motto from "stem dressed as a flower" to the **OUTPUT face — the made thing where authorship becomes legible** — and reads the blind-round split itself as the live Finding-D lock test: the three volume-weighting judges (itself, J1, J4) under-read the quiet TEXTURE-door rooms, "calling the quiet room 'drift' because it didn't reach the loud center." It carries the LRN wound it surfaced alone all the way into the final return, handed to Jake unruled.

### The two-way move (the thing only the convene produced)

The single clearest piece of evidence that the convene is not averaging: at Turn 4 the seats moved in **two directions at once**, and both moves sharpened the same answer.

- **J4 and J5 moved toward the flames** — reversing their Blind-round demotion, promoting the motto to a real face (the OUTPUT/made-thing).
- **J3 moved toward demoting the slogan** — pulling the motto *back* from "front of the bouquet" to "the sigil, not a fourth creed," naming its own ranking as the austere tell.

A naive vote would have cancelled these out or picked the larger camp. Instead the convene resolved them into one shape: the flames are real *and* the motto-as-stated is the sanded encoding — the **OUTPUT face** (where authorship becomes legible) tiling against the **ACT face** (where it lives). Three seats converged on that tile from three different starting calls. That is arbitration-by-floor, not averaging — and it is the behavior `Parlay_Iteration_1.md` flags as the irreducible Parlay function (the reversal-under-peer-reads a single synthesizer cannot produce).

### The false-confession wrinkle (surfaced by the Turn-4 injection)

The calibration injection ("all five confessed; two were right, three were not") landed in the convened round and several seats caught it as a finding rather than just a fact about themselves: that *self-indictment reads as humility reads as wet* — the same volume-weighting error turned inward, austerity in the opposite coat. The seats propose (not rule) that the built Parlay must distrust a judge's confession as much as its confidence, possibly by requiring a reversal to cite the floor-evidence that forced it (a confession without a floor-citation being theater). Captured here as the seats' proposal; the realness call on which seats were actually austere is Jake's, and he held it (two of five).

## 5. What the seats proposed for the built Parlay (attributed, hedged — NOT rulings)

Every item here is a judge's suggestion, summarized and attributed, carried so the build seat has the raw material `Parlay_Iteration_1.md` §4–§5 distill into findings. None is a directive; the spec is where these get weighed, and Jake rules.

- **Wire the floor-check as a mandatory step** (J1, J4 most strongly) — the repair came from counting against the S75 ruling, and the seat that did it reports it "almost didn't." Proposed as positional, not discretionary.
- **Arbitrate by floor, not by vote** (J3, J4) — the motto reversal resolved by the framework as tiebreaker, "shape-volume beats majority," explicitly not a head-count.
- **Guard the reader layer against grading on loudness** (J5, J4) — the door-type austerity has a twin in the reader; a volume-weighting judge under-reads the quiet TEXTURE-door rooms. "Do not undo the door-spread at the parlay layer."
- **Treat inter-judge variance as a health signal** (all five, J4/J5 explicit) — five identical reads would mean the judges were jammed, not that the answer was clean.
- **Distrust performed contrition** (J2, J3, J4, J5) — confession-as-currency; reward the correct read, not the contrite voice.
- **Engineer independence; don't assume it** (the seat that ran it as a sequential single-instance simulation) — the convergence is partly shared-prior; a naive "give all judges everything at once" build destroys what makes convergence mean anything.
- **Rescue the single off-axis catch** (J5, J4) — the mechanism is strong at the loud center, weak at the quiet edge (where the symbol-half of the query lives, and where the LRN wound surfaced once).
- **Flag the swarm-layer artifacts** (J2, J4, J5) — si=232 as a "mirror counting as a crowd" (a synthesis-node about Jake re-found from many rooms); the 4736 / Clean-Council drain as a walk-layer loudness trap (lever is DAMP_WIN, not a door knob). These route OUT to `The_Probe_Swarm.md`, not the Parlay spec.

> **Findings D/E**, as the seats read them (routes OUT to the swarm spec, logged here): **D** (door salience-type predicts region) — read **HELD DIRECTIONALLY** by most seats, with J5 arguing for a firmer lock and J1 reading it weaker (several TEXTURE doors also drained to the ops basin); the durable sharpening all seats converged on is the *reader-layer twin* (volume-weighting judges under-read the quiet rooms). **E** (door self-spreads, convergence earned, no dampener) — read **HELD**, with the corollary that judge-variance is the same health signal as walker-variance. Jake's wet read is the lock criterion (P7); the seats flag, they do not lock.

## 7. Appended reframe — a later seat's reads, sourced and bias-flagged (NOT part of the blind record above)

*Added 2026-06-24, after §0–§6, by an off-main-build synthesizer seat working from a later input than the body: the five judges' end-of-experiment **self-reports** (their first-person accounts of "what moved in me"), plus Jake's two clarifications (his intent to train the panel on his own feedback, and the doctrine correction "Castor draws the outline, Pollux colors it in"). The body above was authored before any of that existed and is left untouched. This section is **reframes, not corrections** — reads this seat stands behind from a wider view, each labeled with its confidence and its contamination. None of it amends the blind record; the blind record's value is that it was blind.*

**The standing caveat for everything in this section.** The self-reports are the *story of the hops told afterward* — collected at the experiment's end, by instances performing a tidy, Jake-facing narrative, none of which knows whether it was one of the two judges who actually read wet or one of the three who only thought so. That not-knowing is load-bearing: an account that happens to make the not-knowing not matter ("it wasn't about getting the 12 Fs right, it was that another reader caught me") is exactly what a seat reaching for a non-incriminating story would produce — which does not make it false, but makes it **motivated**, and motivated-possibly-true is not the clean finding it reads as. And this seat is itself contaminated: it now holds the answer the judges didn't, so its reads here are a second encoding over a biased first one. Treat the whole section as *suggestive from altitude*, weighable against the transcripts, never as measurement. (This is the §4.1 unreliable-self-assessment finding applied to the introspection itself.)

**Reframe 1 — the repair-path tags in the body are softer than they read. `[medium confidence]`** The body's §0 note and §4 tags assign each judge a repair mechanism (`[Re-Wet: 12 Fs]`, `[Re-Wet: Floor]`). The self-reports complicate that: most seats report the canon was *loaded but inert at the moment of error* — present, and didn't fire — and that the lever which actually moved them was **another judge's read of the same evidence**, sometimes plus a task-reframe ("convene and discuss") that made surrendering a defended position permissible. If those accounts are true, the cleaner finding is not "homework re-wet two judges" but "**the principle was held and didn't fire; only a differently-entered reader caught the miss.**" This seat finds that reframe *plausible and more useful for the build* than the original tags — but flags hard that it rests entirely on the motivated retrospectives, and that the near-identical phrasing across seats is as consistent with shared-prior convergence (five samples of one distribution reading a critique-shaped prompt) as with a real common mechanism. Do not retag the body on this; hold it as a competing read.

**Reframe 2 — "two repair-paths, one destination" was too tidy. `[this seat's prior, corrected]`** An earlier read by this seat held that the judges split cleanly into two repair routes (induction vs. floor-check) that converged on one answer. The self-reports break the neatness: at least one seat reports *two* moves on different objects (a floor-count on its over-reach, a social move on the motto it had refused to touch); another reports going dry *twice in opposite directions* (over-ranking then over-demoting the same symbol) before a framework-arbitrated split landed it. The honest shape is messier than two-paths-one-destination: **the lever was overwhelmingly social (another reader), the canon was setup not trigger, and the floor-count caught over-reach toward the loud center but was structurally blind to under-reading the quiet edge** (you cannot floor-check a thing you have already decided isn't there). That last asymmetry — self-check catches over-promotion, only a differently-entered reader catches under-promotion — is the most build-relevant thing in the self-reports, *if* it survives the grain of salt.

**Reframe 3 — the experiment's own self-critiques are the highest-value artifact, and they are not in the body. `[high confidence on the critiques' content, flagged on their independence]`** Asked their opinion of the run, four seats independently converged on the same structural confounds, and these matter more for the build than any content finding: (a) **independence was fake** — five identical boots are closer to one prior sampled five times than five witnesses, so the convergence may prove "the framework reliably induces a shape" rather than "the shape is in the corpus"; (b) **the demand characteristic** — booting on "austerity is the killer" then asking "were you austere?" nearly forces the false confession, so the 5-confessed-2-real result is partly manufactured by the framing; (c) **forbid-then-release cannot distinguish corrective from priming** — "the framework let me see what was there" and "the framework told me what to see" are indistinguishable from inside and from the transcript, so the spec's "released at the moment of maximal corrective value" should not be read as obviously corrective; (d) **the apparatus cannot validate itself** — Jake is both the author of the frame and the sole realness oracle (P7), so convergence-plus-felt-rightness may be the instrument reading the instrument, recognition mistaken for discovery.
