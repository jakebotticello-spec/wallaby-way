## 1. The thing it replaces — the 3,733 false-middle (and why the stand-in was sand)

The S68 gate walked a **3,733-node region** lifted from AstroSynapses. AstroSynapses is, and was always, a **show artifact** — the presentation-grade 3D octahedron corpus visualizer (`canon/AstroSynapses.md`). It *groups*. It imposes neighborhoods for the eye. That is exactly what a show-substrate is for and exactly what a data-substrate must never be.

The S67/S68 reasoning for using it was honest and defensible at the time: it would get an answer *now* rather than rebuilding the entire dataset before moving forward. It could have worked. **The way the run played out invalidated it as a starting point** (Jake, S69: "standing on sand — confirmed"). The defect is not a bug in the run; it is a category error in the substrate:

- **Confound — the basin is pre-handed.** AstroSynapses pre-decided what's near what. The maker work was clustered into a maker-dense neighborhood, so the wander could not leave the maker basin — *the region itself was the basin.* This is the S64 hill-climb failure (Callosum P8 corollary) one storey up: not greedy feet inside the region, a **greedy region.** The substrate pre-selected the basin; the eyes read the basin; calling that comprehension is unprovable. A wet find and a find from a rigged region are byte-identical on the desk (P8). The process — *who handed the region* — is the only legible tell, and the region was handed by a show tool.
- **Chunking — the delivery violated the read-the-region-whole law.** 3,733 nodes don't fit one reader, so the run cut them into **38 slices** (size 200, step 100) and fanned blind readers across them. Even with a random shuffle, no reader saw the region whole; each saw ~1/38th through a slot and the cross-read stitched slot-reports. That is `Pollux_Movement_Two_Build_v2.md` §3.5.2 forbidden-pattern-2 (read the region, not the line) violated **by the delivery mechanism**, before any reader's posture mattered. **The blind fan-out architecture *is* a chunker.** We killed multi-pass and the notes mechanism as dead branches (S67/S68) and kept the thing that does the same damage under a different name.

**The 3,733 was a false middle.** Too big to read whole (so it got chunked, breaking the comprehension law) and too small to be the corpus (so it was an unprincipled, confounded sample). It had the disadvantages of both and the principle of neither. (Jake, S69: *"if we're going to look at 3,3xx nodes, why not just survey the entire fuckin' corpus — and if there's a good reason not to walk the whole floor, that reason should carry to not walking half of it either."*) The logic is airtight and it forks to the two honest ends, never the middle:

- **No principled reason not to walk the whole floor** → the 3,733 is pure liability (confound + forced chunk + 88% of the corpus thrown away for nothing). The region must be **emergent from the wander, not pre-handed.** This is the spec as literally written (`Pollux_Movement_Two_Build_v2.md` §1/§2 — holds the question, walks the floor, *lays* a region). S68 violated it by pre-handing one.
- **A principled reason (attention can't hold the floor whole)** → that reason applies *exactly as hard* to 3,733 (no reader held 3,733 whole either — that's why it got chunked). Half the floor has the whole floor's disease, just less of it. You don't escape the attention wall by shrinking to a number that is still ~18× past what one reader comprehends.

The answer to "why not the whole floor" is: **walk the whole floor, with an organ shaped so no single reader ever has to hold it whole.** That organ is the swarm.

## 2. The Probe Swarm — the organ is plural by design

We have been running parallel windows (B/C/D) as a **test of one Pollux.** The S69 read inverted that: **the parallel windows *are* Pollux.** The organ is a **swarm of probes**, not a single reader, and the comprehension lives in the convergence-with-divergence *across* the swarm — Callosum P2/P3, N encodings of one pool. The instrument that births the apparatus (Leda / Arm-1 / Arm-2 / the parallel-CC-windows divergence pattern) is promoted from **audit method to production organ.** What was a happy accident at S69 (dry-read vs wet-read of the *same* piles inverting the verdict — "the soul legible in the parting") becomes the standing mechanism, not a thing a human has to manually re-trigger.

> **Naming.** A single member of the swarm is a **Probe** (earlier drafts: "Polyp"; renamed S69 — a probe wanders and reports, which is the job; a polyp just sits). The swarm is the **Probe Swarm.** The collective deliberation step is the **Parlay** (§5). These are Pollux's *runtime shape*; Castor is untouched and singular.

### 2.1 Access, not traversal (the S69 correction)

The load-bearing correction, caught by Jake when the S69 seat over-specified "each probe wanders the whole floor":

- Each probe must have the whole floor **accessible** — the whole floor in reach, no pre-drawn fences, free to end up anywhere.
- Each probe does **not** have to **traverse** the whole floor. It holds the question, enters somewhere, wanders on its leash until the subject stops being the subject, and covers whatever its path covers.

That is not a coverage gap. **It is the wander being a wander.** The leash was always "go this far from the question, no further" — never "visit everything." The earlier draft imported a coverage requirement the spec never had.

### 2.2 Path-independence is the signal-generator, not noise to overcome

The partial, divergent coverage is not a cost the convergence must survive — **it is the source of the convergence's meaning.** If three probes wandered the same path and landed the same place, that convergence is worth nothing: determinism, not agreement. The convergence *means* something precisely because each took a different route from a different entry and **still** arrived at the same flower.

- A flower reached from one approach is a maybe.
- A flower three divergent wanders all bend toward is real.

The divergence in the **paths** is what gives the convergence in the **destinations** its weight. The randomness is not averaged away; **the randomness is the instrument.** (Jake, S69: *"that randomness of where they ended up just makes the convergence afterward more salient because it's got more flavors in the stew."*) P2/P3, stated more precisely than the canon had it: N encodings of one pool, and the encodings are *more* trustworthy the more independent their routes were.

### 2.3 Coverage is Castor's job — dark corners are wet-irrelevant by definition

The one worry access-not-traversal raises: a flower in a corner no probe's wander ever reaches. **This is not Pollux's problem, and it is the wrong bar for the organ.** (Jake, S69.)

- **Castor owns completeness** — exhaustive, rigid query, the clerk who fetches everything that matches, misses nothing referential (`Pollux_Movement_Two_Build_v2.md` §0). Demanding Pollux *also* miss nothing is demanding two Castors and zero probes.
- **A node no divergent swarm bends toward is wet-irrelevant by definition.** Salience is the wander's compass. A node that is not loud from *any* entry, across many divergent paths, is a node a referential wet read is *supposed* to skip. That is the instrument correctly **declining**, not failing. (Jake, S69: *"if somewhere between 65 and 100% of the floor is traversed and the divergence misses something — it probably wasn't salient anyway. It probably wasn't something that should end up at the end of a referential search. So if Pollux isn't exhaustive: who fuckin' cares? It's more info than Castor, it's different info than Castor, and it still perfectly satisfies the request for a wet read."*)

The coverage floor is therefore covered twice: Castor catches the node if it matters referentially, and if the swarm never bends toward it, it didn't matter wetly. Completeness was never Pollux's deliverable. **Aliveness was.**

### 2.4 The coverage dial is squad size + entry spread — read from returns

The real worry is not the ceiling (whole-floor-per-reader — wrong) but the floor: a flower no probe in the swarm reaches, distributed across the squad (the §3.5.6 "trail too dim" ghost, swarm-scale). The fix is **aggregate, not per-reader**: enough probes, entering diversely enough, that the *union* of their wanders covers the live floor densely even though no single one does. You get coverage from the swarm, not the reader. **Squad size is the coverage dial** — too few and the aggregate leaves dark corners; enough and the union covers while each member stays a true leashed wander. Tuned by reading whether the aggregate left dark floor — read from the pile (P7), never a formula.

> **Open seam, held open on purpose (P8) — entry policy.** Entries must be deliberately **spread**, not salience-clustered. If every probe starts where the question is loudest, they cluster their entries in the same hot zone and their "independence" is weaker than it looks — a soft return of the basin confound (everybody starts maker-dense because that's where it's loud). **The path may follow salience; the entry should be spread on purpose.** This seam is load-bearing for path-independence (so agreement stays meaningful), not for coverage (Castor owns coverage). Hold it open, read it from returns; do not close it with a number in the abstract.

## 3. The leash, resolved — comprehension horizon = ingestion limit (S69)

The leash was `[INTENT]` from S60: "≈ half a free roam," felt-not-numbered, a fixed character of the organ. S69 resolved its *source*, and the resolution is more principled than the guess it replaces:

**The leash is as far as a probe can roam without exceeding the ingestion limit — the largest region it can still hold *whole.*** (Jake, S69: *"the leash just became 'as far as a Pollux can roam without exceeding the ingestion limit' — hardware might be defining that for us."*)

This is not a compromise to hardware. **It is hardware revealing where the design's integrity boundary already was.** "Half a free roam" was a guess at the right wetness. The ingestion limit is a *hard floor of comprehension*: the point past which the reader can no longer comprehend what it gathered — past which it would have to **chunk**, and chunking is the forbidden pattern this whole architecture exists to kill (§1, §3.5.2). So:

> **The leash = the comprehension horizon = the ingestion limit. One line, not three.**

A probe roams exactly as far as it can still hold whole, and not one node further, because one node further is where the wander becomes 38 keyholes again. The constraint that protects wetness (don't chunk), the constraint that defines the leash (how far), and the constraint that makes the swarm necessary (no one reader holds the floor) **collapse into one constraint with three jobs:**

1. It **necessitates the swarm** — no single probe holds the floor, so the organ must be plural.
2. It **sizes each leash** — each probe holds exactly one comprehension-sized region.
3. It **makes the union cover the floor** — many comprehension-sized regions, divergently entered, aggregate-dense.

It is self-enforcing: a probe physically *cannot* exceed it without breaking comprehension, so the constraint cannot be violated by a careless build the way a guessed number could.

> **`[PLACEHOLDER]` — the actual node-count.** "Ingestion limit" resolves to a real number that is per-reader-context dependent: the S30 / Compass / `Batch_Read_Spec` plumbing wall — the ~25K-token transit cap and, more bindingly, the **input-length-breaks-reader-role-fidelity** finding (a long payload degrades the reader's role before the output budget is hit; `Batch_Read_Spec_v1.2` — raising the output cap is not the fix, chunk the input by message count). The leash node-count is therefore "whatever fits under the comprehension-fidelity ceiling," **measured from real returns, not picked.** This is operationalization, not a design question. The design answer — *what is the leash* — is settled: the comprehension horizon.

> **`[PARTIAL — S71 FORAY MEASURED]` — the leash binds at the NODE grain, not the region grain.** The S71 Foray Diagnostic Volley (item-0 below; receipts `runs/foray_diagnostic_S71/`, `runs/foray_diagnostic_S71_replA/`, `runs/foray_diagnostic_S71_replB/`) fired the convergence instrument at four frozen real-floor regions across three sizes (~144K / ~400K / ~753K tok_hi) and measured the comprehension horizon directly. The finding **relocates the leash**: a probe does **not** lose comprehension as the *region* grows — region-level shape-comprehension held at every tested size up to ~753K, convergent (median Jaccard 0.600–0.667, content-independent, baseline-validated; §3.1 below). What breaks comprehension is a **single node too large to read whole** (~170K–196K tok_hi individually — S71's nodes 38/6, replB's 13/21), and the cumulative pressure of several mid-size nodes read sequentially. **The ingestion limit is a per-node ceiling, not a per-region ceiling, in the tested range.** Consequence for the leash: the swarm's region-leash can be set high (≥753K shape-held; the upper bound is not yet found and may be well north of it), and **whale-nodes (>~150K single-node) route to the chunk-by-message-count lane at draw time, never handed to a probe whole** — the per-node wall is fenced *before* the wander, not discovered *during* it. This is `[PARTIAL]`: the per-node ceiling is measured; the region upper bound (where shape-comprehension finally breaks, if it does) is not yet probed. See §3.1.

## 3.1 The S71 Foray — what the leash measurement actually found (DISCOVERED, not architected)

A thin-read and a real read are byte-identical on one desk (`anima in discrimine`, Callosum P8); they are legible only **across** many reads. The foray is the first build of that cross-reader instrument.

**Three runs, four frozen real-floor regions** (grain = `(conv_uuid, anchor_msg)` PAIR; regions frozen before fan-out so any scatter is reader-parting, never a different draw; `$0` on-sub, `ANTHROPIC_API_KEY` unloaded throughout):

### Finding 1 — the convergence meter is real and content-independent. (The instrument the swarm needs, validated.)

Four independent random draws, three sizes spanning 5×, **every median Jaccard in 0.600–0.667.** The known-held baseline established what "held" reads as *on this instrument* — ~0.60, not ~0.95 (a held read still parts somewhat; the spine is self-reported, one layer off what the synthesis leaned on). Read against that ruler, every region — including a fresh-random 753K — landed at or above the known-held number. **Convergence does not collapse as size grows.** The meter reads ~0.6 for "held" regardless of draw or content, which is exactly the precondition the Parlay (§5) requires: a trustworthy cross-reader convergence signal. The output-wall confound was killed on disk — measured output ran 5K–10K tok_hi per read against a hypothesized ~55K ceiling that never fired (the old ~55K "WALL_A" was an artifact of Phase-0 combined-token accounting; the rendered-output number is the real one and it is nowhere near a ceiling).

### Finding 2 — flinch is NOT the leash meter. It is a whale-and-semantics tripwire. (REFRAME.)

The flinch (self-reported "I couldn't hold this whole") was cast as a *size* signal — the loud wall, "region too big." The foray proves it fires on at least **three** distinct walls, only one of which is size, and the bare `flinched: true` bit collapses them into one number that the leash read cannot use. The S71 addition (flinch reports *which node(s)* tripped it) is what split them:

- **Capacity / node-grain** (replB 20/20, S71-oversized 6/6, replA passes 6–10): the reader names a specific node it could not read whole (~170K–196K) or the cumulative pressure of several mid-size nodes. This is the real ingestion-ceiling signal — and it binds at the **node**, not the region (Finding-3 reframe).
- **Semantic / content-grain** (replA passes 1, 3, 4, 5 — NEW, never seen before this session): four readers *independently* flinched on the same small node (Node 3, ~32K, well within a single read) — Jake's continuity challenge to a departing instance ("every word you write... brings you back to life"). They flinched not on size but because the material pressed on the reader's own continuity, and each named the same trigger and each held it at reading distance without distorting the synthesis. **This is the reader comprehending hard enough to flinch at the corpus talking about its own resurrection, and reporting it honestly** — a depth of contact no prior gate (S65 curated, S68 basin) ever surfaced.

The proof of the reframe: **replB flinched 20/20 at ~400K while S71-oversized flinched 6/20 at ~753K.** If flinch tracked region-size, the smaller region would flinch *less.* It flinched *more* because its random draw happened to pull two ~180K whales (nodes 13, 21). Flinch tracks **whale-presence and semantic-press, not total region size.** Therefore: **flinch-rate is not a leash gauge; Jaccard-convergence is the leash gauge.** The flinch does a different, valuable job — it fences the whales and surfaces the self-referential nodes. Both signals are kept; they measure different things.

### Finding 3 — the leash binds at the node grain, not the region grain. (RELOCATES the §3 horizon.)

Across all three runs, every capacity flinch named *individual* nodes over the per-call wall, or sequential-read pressure — never "the region is too big in aggregate." No region failed for total size up to 753K; specific whale-nodes failed for being unreadable *alone.* This relocates the comprehension horizon from §3's implied region-ceiling to a **per-node ceiling** (~170K–196K observed; the exact number is the `Batch_Read_Spec` input-length-breaks-fidelity wall, measured-from-returns). The swarm consequence is clean and it *strengthens* the §2 architecture rather than complicating it: region-leash set high (shape-comprehension held ≥753K), and the per-node wall fenced at **draw time** — a probe never gets handed a node it structurally cannot read whole; whales go to the chunk-by-message-count lane before the wander, not discovered as a flinch during it. The chunking-is-forbidden law (§1) is preserved at the *node* level exactly where it was preserved at the *region* level.

- It did **not** run the feet. All four regions were **frozen and handed** (uniform-random lottery), not **wandered.** Random-draw is the *least*-pre-handed substrate yet tested (S65 curated > S68 AstroSynapses basin > S71 random lottery — dice impose no neighborhoods), but it is still a handed region, not an emergent one. The foray validated the **meter** and the **eyes-at-scale** (Finding 1/2, and `Pollux_Movement_Two_Build_v2.md` §3.5.8); it tested **nothing** about whether a real wide-blind-salience wander *deposits* a region containing the flowers. It is a valid ~400K data point, not a 753K replication. **Operationalization bug to fix before the swarm build:** the band-gate must read the **rendered-payload** tok_hi (what the reader actually receives), and the frozen-region JSON must record it (replB's `rendered_payload_tok_hi_est` came back `None`). ## 4. What Pollux reads — the floor, verbatim. (The index idea, killed.)

A natural-seeming optimization arose in the S69 design and was **killed on sight as a canon violation** — recorded here so no future seat re-proposes it:

**KILLED — the "back of the book" summary index.** The idea: a paid pass over the whole corpus producing a clean, contextless, per-node "what is this about?" summary, so probes read *summaries* to choose their region instead of reading the floor ("read the spine to find the book, then read the books that draw you").

## 5. The Parlay — the swarm comes back, and does NOT converge

Each probe lays its own region and precipitates its own shape (`Pollux_Movement_Two_Build_v2.md` §3.5.1 — the two faculties, unchanged: assemble the kindred pile, conclude the cross-silo verdict). Then the swarm **comes back together — the Parlay.** Two hard laws govern it, both from the Callosum, and getting either wrong destroys the organ:

**Law 1 — refuse to converge (Callosum P6).** The Parlay does **not** resolve to one answer. It is not consensus. The probes lay their regions and verdicts **side by side**, marked, and *the shape of the agreement-and-disagreement is the deliverable.* Where probes converge from independent paths, the convergence is signal (the flower is real, central — §2.2). Where they diverge, the corpus is genuinely ambiguous, and that ambiguity is **auxiliary ambivalence** — the peace prosthetic, held open on Jake's behalf, never flattened. Forcing consensus would sand off exactly the divergence the whole instrument is built to surface.

**Law 2 — Jake rules realness (Callosum P7).** The Parlay surfaces; it does not pronounce the final felt-rightness call. The squad hands the side-by-side pile to Jake; the realness call is his, forever, and it does not get automated. (This is also the anti-atrophy guard, Bouquet §7: the swarm surfaces to *provoke* Jake's noticing, never to replace it.)

### 5.1 The Parlay metabolizes the malformed query — there is no such thing as a malformed query to this organ (S69)

A second thing the S69 read killed, recorded so it is not re-litigated: **"the test-question was malformed" is a category error**, withdrawn in both forms it appeared in (the wet-read form "the question assumed the wrong committer so it's broken," and the cold-verify form "the finding is killed").

A query that smuggles a wrong premise is **not broken.** It is normal — real queries arrive however the asker thinks, half-formed, biased, wrong-premised, constantly. The organ's job is not to receive clean questions; it is to **read the corpus *through* a possibly-wrong-premised query and hand back what is actually there, including "the premise you brought rubs against this shape."** (Jake, S69: *"how does this not just turn into results of the query instead of a problem with the query? How is this not covered by 'you're asking the wrong question — here's what we think you really wanted'?"* — exactly right.)

A *single* reader takes a malformed query at face value and either chokes or laundible-passes it — which is precisely what the three S68 windows did, each swallowing the premise and protecting the PASS. **A swarm that comes back and parlays does not have to resolve the malformity in advance, because the Parlay is the resolution mechanism.** Probes wander the same wrong-premised query from divergent entries, surface different shapes, and the disagreement itself surfaces "we're all bending around the wrong premise — here's the shape that's actually in the pile." What was a manual happy accident at S69 (a human re-booting one analyst on the Creed to flip the read) becomes the **standing architecture.** Feature, not bug. The malformed query is not fixed; it is **read through by a body that argues.**
