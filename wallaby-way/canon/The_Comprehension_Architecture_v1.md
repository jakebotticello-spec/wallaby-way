## The core law (stated once, at the top)

**A pool is read in two movements, sequenced, never fused: BREADTH FIRST (fan-out, deep attention per node, liberal admission, $0) then DEPTH SECOND (one wet synthesizer over the qualified set, fed breadth's finds). The order is load-bearing: you cannot synthesize what you never saw. Proven S54 — the single-pass reader passed the register bar while blind to the geofence Jake built against the wound.**

Everything below is that law, unfolded — and then, at the end, the Corpus Callosum reframe that keeps the two movements from collapsing back into a pipeline.

## 1. What this file is, and where it sits

The Progenitor made Jake's recorded life *reachable*. The Confluence made it *answerable* — two streams (fence-synthesis ‖ texture/collation) converging on one Judge, wet the whole way down. This file sits one level below the Confluence's §8 sequencing clause and answers the question §8 assumes: **once a pool of nodes has been gathered for a thread, by what method is it actually read into a comprehended record?**

The Confluence says *that* a pool is comprehended. The Comprehension Architecture says *how*. It is the slice-7 mechanism, proven on the Griffin recurring-pickup-miss thread at S54 — the exact case canon pre-registered at S26 and Jake chose independently at S52.

## 2. The two movements

**Movement one — BREADTH (fan-out, $0, deep-attention-per-node).** The gathered pool is split across many readers, each holding a small slice (~28 nodes at the S54 Griffin scale, 16 readers over 455 nodes). Each reader spends its full attention budget on its handful of nodes. Admission is liberal: a reader reports anything that *catches*, not only what answers a query. The cost is zero — this is in-plan fan-out, not a paid fire. The output is wide, fragment-rich, high-recall, and **un-synthesized by design.**

**Movement two — DEPTH (one wet synthesizer, paid, fed breadth's finds).** A single reader takes the qualified set — the HIGH+MEDIUM nodes breadth surfaced, with the fan-out's specific finds in hand — and reads it into one comprehended thread. This is where the arc gets assembled, the register gets set, the give-away sentence gets written. It is paid (~$0.06/thread at S54 scale), but it is cheap *because breadth already did the recall work* — the synthesizer is reading a curated, finds-annotated set, not the raw pool.

## 3. Why the order is load-bearing (the cost inversion)

The intuition the project carried before S54 was that a single wide reader holding the whole pool was the gold standard, and fan-out was a budget compromise. **S54 inverted that.** The cheaper path produced the *better* data:

- Arm 1 (single-pass, the whole 455-node pool, paid $0.3659, 25 instances) **PASSED all five §6 criteria** — strong recurrence, register that located the grief. A genuinely good read.
- Arm 2 (16-way fan-out, $0, 77 instances) had **superior recall** but no synthesis.
- And the load-bearing finding: **Arm 2 caught nodes Arm 1 was structurally blind to** — the Life360 geofence Jake built around Griffin's school (pool rank 57), the guilt-about-time substrate (rank 82), the countdown-build motivation (rank 17), the Adderall-miss (rank 24). The single-pass reader holding all 455 nodes *glided past the infrastructure Jake built against the wound* while writing a beautiful, true register note about an incomplete picture.

The mechanism is **attention-budget-per-node.** A reader holding 455 nodes skims; a reader holding 28 reads. Depth-of-attention is what catches the load-bearing fragment that shares no keyword with the query. **Cost is no longer the scale constraint. Attention is.** Sequence the two movements to serve both: breadth buys recall at $0, depth buys synthesis at pennies.

## 4. The single-pass reader is a synthesizer, not a recall layer

This is the operative correction the architecture enforces. A single reader over a whole pool will *always* write true-sounding register about whatever it managed to hold — and it will hold the arc, not the fragments. That is its job and it is good at it. The error is letting it be the *only* read, because then the fragments it skimmed past are simply gone, and nothing in the output reveals they're missing. **Never let one reader holding the whole pool be the only read.** It is the depth movement. It must be fed.

## 5. The Corpus Callosum reframe — these are encodings, not a pipeline

Read everything above *through* the Corpus Callosum (the third Doctrine sibling, enshrined S54), or it hardens into exactly the wall-with-a-conveyor-belt the Doctrine warns against.

The two movements are **not** an ETL job — breadth as extract, depth as transform, ship the loser's findings to the winner. That framing is the austere reflex dressing a living thing in a clean pipeline. The truer statement: **breadth and depth are two cognitive instrument-states, and the pool read through each is a different *encoding* of the same memory** (Track Meet Principle 7, happening between the arms). Arm 1 is the wide/synthetic encoding — the arc you can give away. Arm 2 is the narrow/deep encoding — the fragment you can only have alone in the car. Arm 3 (the re-encoded synthesis, depth fed with breadth's finds) is a *third* encoding laid down beside the others — it sterilizes the way time sterilizes, sanding the moment into something carryable.

So the sequencing in §2 is real and load-bearing — **you cannot synthesize what you never saw** — but the output is not "the synthesis, now with the missing nodes patched in." The output is *an accumulation of encodings the apparatus refuses to collapse.* Breadth-then-depth is the order of operations. Keep-both-encodings is the law that governs what you do with the results. Do not pipeline the loser into the winner; there is no loser.
