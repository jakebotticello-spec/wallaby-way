## 1. The role — third stream into the pile

Arm 2 produces catches, the same way Blind and Creed produce catches. Its output is a **third provenance stream the collator pours into the pile** alongside Blind-wordless and Creed-full. That pile is what the filter later reads.

The phrase to keep dead: Arm 2 does **not** "warm the substrate." The substrate is the cold floor the pickers wander, and Arm 2 never touches it any differently than the others do — it reads it, blind to what they're doing, exactly as they read it blind to what it's doing. If anything is "warmed," it is the **pile downstream** — the deep-narrow read deposits texture into the collected catch that the wide blind wanders don't produce. That warming happens *after* the collator, in the material the filter sees. It cannot reach back to the floor or to any picker's draw, because Arm 2 and the pickers share no input and never meet until the pile.

**The governing image (Jake's):** the portrait is **outlines drawn by the reference side, colored in by the organic side.** All three pickers are organic-side instruments coloring the corpus in; Arm 2's deep-narrow read is one of the colors. Provenance keeps the brushstrokes labeled so the filter knows which instrument laid down which stem.

## 2. G1 holds for free — Arm 2 shares no input with any picker

G1 (the picker's draw stays blind and unbiased — umbrella §1, §2; Bouquet §4 G1) is not a wall that has to be *built around* Arm 2. It holds **automatically**, because Arm 2 and the pickers were never near each other's inputs to begin with.

> Arm 2 reads the floor on its own draw. Blind reads the floor on its own draw. Creed reads the floor on its own draw. **No instrument's output is any other instrument's input.** They converge only at the collator, in the pile — which is downstream of every draw. So no Arm 2 output *can* reach a picker's draw: there is no path. The same is true in reverse (no Creed output reaches Arm 2's draw) and between the two pickers (no Blind output reaches Creed's draw). G1 is a property of the architecture — three independent inputs, one shared output bin — not a rule policing a special instrument.

This is the correction at the heart of the v2 rewrite. The v1 spec invented an upstream "influence" for Arm 2 and then had to bolt on a wall to contain the influence it had invented. Remove the invented influence and the wall has nothing to do — because the thing it guarded against was never possible under the real model. **What proof is owed at build (§4) is not "build the wall" but "show on disk that the three draws are genuinely independent" — confirming the property, not constructing it.**
