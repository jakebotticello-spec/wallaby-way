## 2. THE FOUR DISCIPLINE CORRECTIONS (the spec changes — these are the value)

### 2.1 query-BIASED, not query-blind  [CORRECTS a print + a walking posture]

The init prints `*** QUERY SET DOWN. Walk proceeds query-blind from here. ***`. **That wording
is wrong and it cost the first walk.** Reading it as "blind" collapsed Pollux toward Leda: the
first walk took the entry door, latched a floor-precipitated through-line (the self-model), and
followed THAT thread's internal logic — calling on-query nodes "drift" when they left the
latched thread (it declined si=6594, a literal operating-creed, as off-thread).

**The correction (Jake):** Pollux is QUERY-BIASED. Castor = the dictionary answer. Pollux = the
book of poetry ABOUT the subject. Leda = blind, no question at all. The query is "set down" only
in the §1/S63 sense — set down the *retrieval gate / computed seed* — NOT set down as the
reading stance. The question stays the **color** the whole walk; the leash fires on leaving the
QUERY's subject (the building), not on leaving a sub-thread (one room).

**Impact on the run:** scrapped the first 3-node walk entirely, re-init'd, re-walked from the
same entry holding "creeds/through-lines" as live color every step. The re-walk immediately
diverged correctly — it stopped tunneling the self-model strand and started spanning the
building's rooms.

**Spec action:** change the init print `query-blind` → `query-held (biased)`. And state in the
WET BOOT, even more explicitly than it does, that the question is the building the walk stays
inside, and the leash is the building's wall — not the current node's neighborhood.

### 2.2 Pollux is a REFERENTIAL query-finder — walk THROUGH the dry aisle to reach the poetry  [CORRECTS leash-as-stop-on-first-thinning]

The corrected-but-still-too-timid walk hit a neighborhood of pure catalog-ops nodes and read
"this is operations, not creeds → drift → stop." Wrong. **(Jake):** the RETURN is poetry, but
the READ is referential. Sometimes you walk *through* the section with the encyclopedias and
dictionaries to *get to* the poetry section. Walking through dry ground ≠ harvesting it; you
don't deposit the dictionaries, but you don't refuse to traverse them either.

**Impact:** instead of stopping at the catalog-ops thinning, the walk traversed it — and the
REFUSED-wall creed (5883) was sitting on the far side of that dry aisle, inside the
catalog-dig conversation cluster, behind an ops-titled node. Refusing the aisle = never reaching
the flower.

**Spec action:** the leash is a DRIFT BUDGET over the *subject* (does the building's subject
still hold), not a stop-trigger on the first dry/ops neighborhood. Dry ground inside the
building is corridor, not edge.

### 2.3 WALK-THE-EXTENT then harvest-the-most-salient-on-the-way-back  [SPLITS leash-as-stop from leash-as-boundary]

**(Jake's theory, tested live):** don't stop at the first felt edge. Walk the FULL extent of
the leash, THEN come back and harvest the most salient in-building material encountered along
the way. This splits two things the S72 build fused: leash-as-stop-trigger and
leash-as-region-boundary. A felt edge might be a LOCAL DIP that re-opens two hops further; you
can't know without walking past it.

**Impact (this is the load-bearing one):** the walk hit a felt edge at step 3's neighborhood,
logged "I'd stop here," then walked PAST it on Jake's ruling. Walking past it:
- PROVED one edge real (si=3079's neighborhood was pure catalog-ops, the dip did NOT re-open
  toward creeds — the stop-call was confirmed by walking, not guessed), AND
- OVERTURNED a different edge when the abyss paid out (§2.4) — the REFUSED-wall creed was past
  a thinning the timid walk would have stopped at.

So walk-the-extent earned its keep in BOTH directions in one run: it confirmed a real edge and
overturned a false one. The harvest-on-the-way-back is the safety net that makes walking past a
felt edge affordable — you're scouting, not committing the region.

**Spec action:** the walk does not stop at the first thinning. It walks to the extent of the
subject-leash, logging felt edges as data, and the region is what genuinely caught IN THE
BUILDING along the way — not everything up to the first stop. (Pollux.md §2 already frames the
leash as a drift-BUDGET; this operationalizes "budget" as walk-the-extent-then-harvest.)

### 2.4 ABYSS-RISK — deliberately take the node DISconnected from the center of mass  [the cross-domain-rhyme move, made a route policy]

**(Jake):** with harvest-on-the-way-back making node-choice safe, the walk can take RISKS — pick
the node obviously NOT connected to the center of mass you're standing in, wander into the abyss,
and see if it finds relevancy along the EDGES later. The low-cosine cross-domain rhyme (Leda
§1.4, AstroSynapses §4) is reachable ONLY by multi-hop, never a direct near-edge. The safe walk
follows near-edges and stays in-domain; the abyss-jump deliberately leaves the near-graph to see
if a far node loops back to the subject through a door nobody would have drawn.

**Impact:** at si=3079 the walk passed the loud rank-1 (si=2414, "D10-D13 decisions," loudness
12.5, the dictionary) and took si=5883 ("ignition script... leads with REFUSED wall," loudness
8.5) — the content-is-a-creed stranger over the loud near-node. That stranger carried the
REFUSED wall in SUBSTANCE (Jake's own voice building the wall). **The single best flower of the
run came from the abyss-pick.** It is on the landed path, deposited, ruled real by Jake.

**Spec action + the discipline that keeps it Pollux-not-Leda:** the abyss-jump is RECONNAISSANCE,
deposited CONDITIONALLY. Take the risky route; deposit only if, on reading the node (or its
neighbors), it actually rhymes back to the query's subject. A node that's "out there" and STAYS
out there is logged scouted-and-empty, not deposited. The RISK is in the ROUTE; the HARVEST
stays disciplined to in-building. That boundary is what keeps walk-the-abyss from collapsing into
Leda (blind, no question).

**Open design fork for Jake (NOT resolved here):** is entry-Castor-drop correct, or does the

## 3. THE THREE BUILD BUGS (surfaced by the landed artifact — JOB-0-class fixes for the next build)

### 3.1 `finalize` KeyError: 'salience'  [CRASH — non-fatal, post-write]
`cmd_finalize` (line ~910) writes `walk_report_S72.md` by looping ALL provenance records doing
`p["salience"]`. The 1-hop off-path (hop1) records added during expansion never get a `salience`
key (only stepped-to path nodes do). Crash. **It is post-write** — `region_S72.json`,
`provenance_S72.json`, `toks_S72.json` all landed first; only the .md tail crashed (and even it
wrote partially). **Fix:** `p["salience"]` → `p.get("salience", "—")`. The honest representation:
hop1 nodes were NEVER walked-to, they have no felt salience — render them with a dash, not a
fake tag. Same class as the freeze-JSON bug: a write-path assuming uniform record shape across
two populations (path vs hop1).

### 3.2 `path_nodes` excludes the Castor-dropped entry — report should SAY so  [miscount trap]
`path_nodes: 2` is correct (entry Castor-dropped) but it silently invites the exact over-count
the OC made live ("three rooms"). **Fix:** the region JSON + report should carry an explicit
`entry_castor_dropped: true` and a one-line note ("entry node si=6075 delivered by Castor, not
in Pollux path"), so a reader counts the region right without re-deriving it. Tie to the §2.5
fork — if Jake rules the entry should be carried, this changes; if dropped stays, the note is
the fix.

### 3.3 `total_tok_hi_est: 1,600,418` is meaningless — it sums the hop1 skirt  [honesty-number bug]
The 1.6M total sums ALL 21 nodes including 100K-267K hop1 nodes that were NEVER READ (they're
addressed off-path skirt, fetched for msg_uuids only). The REAL read-region is the 2 path nodes
the probe comprehended: 8,188 + 9,807 = ~18K tok. **Fix:** report TWO numbers — `read_region_tok`
(path nodes only, what was comprehended) and `addressed_skirt_tok` (hop1 bulk, never read) —
never one conflated total. Exact same defect class as the freeze-JSON `total_csv_tok_hi` vs
`rendered_payload_tok_hi_est` honesty gap (JOB 0, S72). A field's name must match what it counts.

  The corpus has no dedicated "here is my creed" nodes; the creeds are Jake's closing

  small flowers. This is a real structural property of where creeds are shelved — load-bearing
  for the eyes-gate and the swarm coverage math.
- **One probe, one entry, one query.** Everything here is N=1. The swarm (spread entries, Parlay)
